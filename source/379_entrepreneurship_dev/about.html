About the Entrepreneurship Development Programme

Our mission, vision, and approach

The Entrepreneurship Development Programme exists to help founders and intrapreneurs turn validated ideas into sustainable, investable ventures. We combine structured learning, experienced mentoring, and targeted programme design to meet entrepreneurs where they are.

Our vision is a connected community of founders who can access the right support at the right time—whether that is a first incubator experience, a fast-paced accelerator, or focused funding preparation. Every programme is built around practical application, peer learning, and exposure to real-world investors and operators.

We work closely with regional partners, industry experts, and the wider TUS ecosystem to ensure that our portfolio of programmes and support offerings stays relevant, inclusive, and outcomes-driven for founders across sectors and backgrounds.

Programme types within the portfolio

Our portfolio spans different programme formats so that founders can engage at the depth and pace that fits their context. While each initiative has its own design, programmes broadly cluster into incubator-style experiences, accelerator-style sprints, and funding-focused tracks.

Incubator-style programmes typically support earlier-stage founders who are validating a problem-solution fit, building an initial team, or preparing their first pilots. Accelerator-style programmes are designed for ventures with early traction that want to sharpen their growth strategy and investor story. Funding tracks focus on capital readiness, from grants and equity-free support to investor-led equity programmes.

The mix of formats, schedules, and delivery modes (online, in-person, and hybrid) is informed by live data from our active programmes and cohorts. This gives you flexibility to engage alongside work, study, or existing venture commitments.

Programme categories at a glance

Complementary learning and support

Alongside formal programmes, founders can access shorter learning experiences, events, and one-to-one mentoring. These are designed to fill specific skill gaps, provide just-in-time advice, and create regular touchpoints with the entrepreneurial community.

Courses provide structured, topic-specific learning on areas such as market validation, startup finance, fundraising, and leadership. Events offer live opportunities to learn from practitioners, test ideas, and expand your network. Mentoring connects you with experienced founders and operators who can provide context-aware guidance on your venture journey.

You can combine these offerings with a core programme, or use them independently as your needs evolve over time.

Who the programme is for

The Entrepreneurship Development Programme is designed for founders and intrapreneurs across a range of stages—from those validating an early idea to teams preparing to scale. Rather than a one-size-fits-all offer, we use your stage and goals to help you prioritise the most relevant programmes and support.

Some founders join while still in full-time employment or study, exploring whether entrepreneurship is the right path. Others already have customers and revenue, and need focused support to systemise growth, raise capital, or professionalise operations.

Use the selector alongside this text to choose the description that best matches your situation. You will see tailored guidance and suggested next steps that you can follow immediately.

Expected outcomes and next steps

Founders who engage fully with the Entrepreneurship Development Programme typically leave with a clearer venture thesis, a validated customer problem, and a concrete plan for their next 12–18 months. Many also progress towards key funding milestones, stronger founding teams, and more robust operational practices.

Across our programmes and support offerings, we emphasise measurable outcomes: sharper value propositions, testable go-to-market strategies, investor-ready materials, and a stronger personal leadership toolkit for each founder.

Your next step will depend on your stage. You can explore the full programme portfolio, dive into standalone courses, connect with mentors, or reach out directly to discuss the best route through the ecosystem.
