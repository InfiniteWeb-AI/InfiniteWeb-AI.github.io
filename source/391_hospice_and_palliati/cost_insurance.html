Cost & Insurance

Estimate home palliative care costs in clear terms

This page explains, in everyday language, how hospice and palliative care are usually covered. You can also use the Home Palliative Care Cost Estimator to try out numbers and save estimates to review later.

How hospice & palliative care are usually covered

Many families are surprised to learn that hospice and palliative care are often well-covered by existing health insurance. Exact coverage varies by plan, but most people do not pay the full cost out of pocket.

Medicare. For people who qualify for Medicare, hospice care is typically covered under the Medicare hospice benefit. This often includes nursing visits, medication related to comfort, basic medical equipment (such as a hospital bed), and support from social workers and chaplains.

Medicaid. Many state Medicaid programs offer hospice coverage that is similar to Medicare. Details depend on your state, but it commonly covers nursing visits, supplies, and support services.

Private insurance. Commercial or employer plans often cover hospice or home-based palliative care. Some plans mirror Medicare benefits; others have copays, deductibles, or visit limits. Your insurer can explain your specific benefits.

Self-pay and other programs. If a service is not fully covered, families may choose to pay privately, or they may qualify for financial assistance funds or community support programs.

The estimator below is a planning tool. It is not a guarantee of benefits or a bill. A nurse or financial counselor can review your exact coverage and provide a personalized explanation.

At a glance

  • Most hospice patients have little or no out-of-pocket cost for core services.
  • Coverage depends on your primary payer (Medicare, Medicaid, private insurance, self-pay, or other).
  • Some items (for example, non-formulary medications or extra equipment) may have additional costs.
  • The Home Palliative Care Cost Estimator shows approximate costs for planning and conversation with your care team.
  • For an exact explanation of benefits, please call your insurance plan or speak with our admissions team.

The numbers you see in the estimator are educational only and do not replace a detailed benefits review.

Home Palliative Care Cost Estimator

Use this tool to estimate the total and daily cost of home palliative nursing visits based on how long you expect services, your primary payer, and how many nurse visits you prefer each week.

For example, enter "14" and choose "days" for about two weeks, or "3" and choose "months" for a longer course.

If you are not certain, choose "Other or not sure". The estimate will still provide a rough range.

Your care team can help you decide how many visits are appropriate. You can adjust this later.
