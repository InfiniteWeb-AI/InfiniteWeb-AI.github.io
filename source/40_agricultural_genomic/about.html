Enabling data-driven breeding decisions worldwide

We exist to help breeding programs of every size turn genomic insight into improved varieties — faster, more predictably, and at scale.

From national agricultural research systems to private seed companies, breeders are under pressure to deliver resilient, high-yielding varieties in increasingly variable environments. Our role is to make advanced genomics and digital breeding tools practical, affordable, and tightly integrated with the way your teams already work in the field and in the lab.

  • Bridge genomics and agronomy by connecting marker data, phenotypes, and environmental information into a single decision framework.
  • Accelerate genetic gain through workflows that support marker-assisted selection, genomic selection, and rapid cycling strategies.
  • Strengthen local capacity via training, implementation support, and tools that adapt to diverse infrastructure and connectivity contexts.

Scientific and technical expertise

Our team combines molecular genetics, quantitative breeding, bioinformatics, and software engineering to deliver end-to-end agricultural genomics and breeding solutions.

Genotyping and marker platforms

We support a range of marker systems and genotyping densities, from targeted marker sets for routine quality control to higher-density marker configurations for genomic selection and discovery. Assays are designed with breeders to balance information content, cost per sample, and operational throughput.

  • Panel design aligned to trait, crop, and population structure.
  • Sample logistics planning for multi-location, multi-season pipelines.
  • Robust quality control to ensure consistent marker performance.

Sequencing and discovery workflows

For programs investing in discovery and pre-breeding, we help design sequencing strategies that generate high-value insights without overwhelming downstream analysis. This includes targeted sequencing for key loci, as well as broader profiling for diversity analysis and marker discovery.

  • Study design for diversity panels, mapping populations, and reference sets.
  • Variant calling and curation pipelines tailored to crop and genome complexity.
  • Data structures that stay compatible with routine breeding workflows.

Breeding data and decision support software

We design software workflows that respect how breeders actually work: organizing germplasm, planning trials, capturing phenotypes, and making advancement decisions under time and resource constraints.

  • Germplasm and pedigree management tailored to complex breeding histories.
  • Trial design and data capture workflows for field, greenhouse, and lab.
  • Analytics and reporting views that surface the right comparisons at the right time.

Implementation, training, and change management

Adopting genomics-informed breeding is as much about people and process as it is about technology. We work alongside breeding teams to co-design implementation plans that are realistic for their staffing, infrastructure, and funding landscape.

  • Workflow mapping from current practice to target genomics-enabled pipelines.
  • Role-specific training for breeders, technicians, data managers, and leadership.
  • Ongoing support to adapt workflows as programs grow or priorities shift.

Collaborations and regional focus

We collaborate with breeding programs, seed companies, and research consortia across multiple regions. Each collaboration is built around local priorities, infrastructure realities, and timelines.

Our work spans tropical and temperate cropping systems, smallholder and commercial value chains, and both public and private sector breeding initiatives. We adapt our support model to local needs — whether that means helping a team run its first marker-assisted selection cycle, or integrating genomic selection into an established pipeline.

In every region, we aim to be a long-term partner: co-designing studies, aligning sampling and trialing plans, and building the local capacity needed to sustain genomics-enabled breeding beyond any single project.

Focus by region

Global programs

Globally, we support cross-cutting initiatives that connect breeding programs across continents, enabling shared marker sets, harmonized data structures, and comparable trial designs. This helps teams learn from one another while still tailoring decisions to local environments.

  • Coordinated marker strategies and data standards.
  • Benchmarking of workflows across crops and regions.
  • Shared training materials and implementation playbooks.

Africa

In African programs, we often focus on integrating genomics into resource-constrained environments, where field operations must be resilient to logistical and infrastructure challenges.

  • Genotyping strategies that respect seasonal cash flow and sample logistics.
  • Support for multi-environment trials capturing stress-prone environments.
  • Capacity building for local data management and analysis teams.

Asia

Across Asia, breeding programs often manage high population sizes and intensive multi-season trialing. We help teams streamline decision points and prioritize where genomics adds the most value.

  • Marker-assisted backcrossing workflows for trait introgression.
  • Genomic selection pipelines aligned with large-scale trial networks.
  • Decision dashboards for rapid advancement and productization.

Europe

European collaborators frequently operate within highly digitized environments with strong regulatory frameworks. Our work emphasizes interoperability, data governance, and integration with existing infrastructures.

  • Interfacing breeding data systems with enterprise data platforms.
  • Traceability of germplasm, samples, and breeding decisions.
  • Analytics to support sustainability and quality traits.

North America

In North America, we often collaborate with teams running large commercial pipelines that must balance speed, risk, and product performance across diverse environments.

  • Integration of genomic predictions into advancement and culling decisions.
  • Workflows to manage large trial networks and historical data assets.
  • Scenario analysis for pipeline design and resource allocation.

South America

South American collaborations frequently center on high-intensity production systems, with a focus on traits linked to abiotic stress, disease resistance, and grain quality.

  • Marker strategies tuned to local disease and pest pressures.
  • Trialing designs for diverse production environments and sowing windows.
  • Deployment planning for new varieties across heterogeneous markets.

Oceania

In Oceania, breeding programs often operate across contrasting environments with strong emphasis on water use efficiency, grain quality, and climate resilience.

  • Genomic tools for managing genotype-by-environment interactions.
  • Data integration from long-term trials and environmental monitoring.
  • Decision frameworks that support both research and commercial objectives.

Other regions and emerging programs

We also support emerging programs and cross-regional initiatives that do not fit neatly into a single geography, including pre-breeding collaborations and germplasm exchange networks.

  • Pilot projects to demonstrate value of genomics in new crops or regions.
  • Shared reference populations and marker sets across institutions.
  • Knowledge exchange and peer learning across partner networks.

How our solutions fit into breeding workflows

We design solutions around real breeding workflows, from germplasm management to variety deployment, so that genomic information supports — rather than disrupts — the way your teams make decisions.

  1. 1. Germplasm strategy and population design

    Work begins with clarifying objectives and designing populations that can deliver the genetic variation and statistical power you need, whether for discovery or routine selection. We help align germplasm management, pedigree tracking, and crossing plans with downstream genotyping and analysis.

  2. 2. Trial design and phenotyping

    Robust phenotypes remain the foundation of effective genomics. We support trial designs and data capture workflows that make it straightforward to connect field, greenhouse, and lab data to genotypes across seasons and locations.

  3. 3. Genotyping and sequencing operations

    Together we plan sampling, shipping, and genotyping or sequencing runs so that data arrive in time for key decision points. Marker sets and sequencing strategies are chosen to match your objectives — from simple quality control checks to genomic selection cycles.

  4. 4. Data management and analysis

    Data from trials, laboratories, and external sources are organized into consistent structures. Analytics workflows — including predictions, indices, and reporting views — are configured so breeders can move from raw data to clear recommendations within their standard timelines.

  5. 5. Advancement, productization, and deployment

    Finally, genomics and analytics are embedded into advancement meetings, product selection, and deployment planning. This ensures that new varieties reflect both genetic potential and the realities of target production environments and markets.
