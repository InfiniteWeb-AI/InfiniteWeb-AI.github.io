    AgriGenomics Privacy Policy

    This Privacy Policy explains how AgriGenomics Solutions ("AgriGenomics", "we", "us") collects, uses, stores, and shares personal data in connection with our agricultural genomics and breeding products, services, software, and digital resources.

    Effective date
    1 January 2026
    Policy version
    2026.1

    This Privacy Policy applies when you visit our websites (including agrigenomics example domains), use our software products (such as breeding management tools), engage with our genomics and sequencing services, participate in training, or otherwise interact with us where we act as a controller of your personal data.

    Some services, such as contractual sequencing or breeding projects, may be subject to additional privacy or data processing terms agreed with your organization. In case of conflict, those contractual terms will prevail over this general Privacy Policy.

    We process personal data in accordance with applicable data protection laws, which may include the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR, and other local regulations in the regions where we operate.

    We collect personal data directly from you, from your organization, and automatically through your use of our digital services. The specific data we collect depends on your relationship with us, but may include:

    • Contact and identity data — such as name, institutional affiliation, role, email address, postal address, telephone number, and login identifiers.
    • Account and subscription data — such as usernames, authentication credentials (stored securely), account preferences, access logs, and subscription details for software or services.
    • Professional information — such as your research or breeding focus, crops of interest, training history, and project information you choose to share with us.
    • Transaction and billing data — such as purchase history, quotes, invoices, payment status, and tax-related information. Payment card details are processed via compliant payment providers and are not stored by us in full.
    • Service usage data — such as log files, device identifiers, browser information, IP address, approximate location, referral URLs, and interactions with our websites and applications.
    • Support and communication data — such as the content of emails, support tickets, feedback forms, and other communications with us.

    We do not intentionally collect personal data about end-consumers of crop products, nor do we use genomic or phenotypic data relating to breeding lines or samples as personal data unless such data can reasonably identify an individual person.

    We use personal data for the following purposes:

    • Providing products and services — to create and manage accounts, fulfil orders for genotyping panels and sequencing services, configure software subscriptions, deliver training, and provide customer support.
    • Operating and improving our platforms — to maintain the security, availability, and performance of our websites, software, and infrastructure, and to develop new features based on aggregated usage insights.
    • Customer relationship management — to respond to inquiries, manage contracts, send service communications, and coordinate projects such as breeding trials or assay design.
    • Compliance and risk management — to comply with legal obligations, audit requirements, and industry standards, and to prevent fraud, misuse, or security incidents.
    • Research, analytics, and reporting — to analyse anonymised or aggregated usage patterns, measure performance of our offerings, and report on service quality to customers and partners.
    • Marketing with your consent or as permitted by law — to send relevant information about new panels, services, software features, or educational resources. You can opt out of such communications at any time.

    Where GDPR or similar laws apply, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases to process your personal data:

    • Contract performance — when processing is necessary to enter into or perform a contract with you or your organization, for example to provide sequencing services, software access, or training.
    • Legitimate interests — when processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests (or those of a third party) and your interests and fundamental rights do not override those interests. These may include ensuring service security, improving our offerings, and managing customer relationships.
    • Consent — when you have given us clear consent for specific processing activities, such as receiving certain marketing communications. You can withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior lawful processing.
    • Legal obligations — when processing is required to comply with applicable laws, regulations, or judicial orders.

    When we rely on legitimate interests, we balance them against your rights and expectations and implement safeguards to protect your privacy.

    We may share personal data in the following circumstances:

    • Within AgriGenomics group entities — on a need-to-know basis for purposes consistent with this policy, such as global support, finance, and infrastructure operations.
    • With service providers — including IT hosting, cloud platforms, payment processors, logistics providers, and professional advisors who process data on our behalf under appropriate contractual safeguards.
    • With project partners — when we jointly deliver projects such as breeding trials or capacity-building initiatives, and where data sharing is necessary and governed by agreements with your organization.
    • Legal and regulatory disclosure — when required by law, regulation, or valid legal process, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of AgriGenomics, our customers, or others.

    As a global company, we may transfer personal data across national borders, including to countries that may not provide the same level of data protection as your home jurisdiction. When doing so, we implement appropriate safeguards, such as the European Commission's standard contractual clauses or equivalent mechanisms, in accordance with applicable law.

    Our websites and online tools use cookies and similar technologies (such as web beacons and local storage) to provide core functionality, understand how our services are used, and improve user experience.

    We generally group these technologies into:

    • Strictly necessary cookies — required for basic site functionality, security, and network management. These cannot be switched off in our systems.
    • Analytics and performance cookies — helping us measure traffic, understand feature usage, and diagnose performance issues. Where required, we deploy these only with your consent.
    • Functional cookies — enabling enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering preferences or session state across visits.

    You can manage cookie preferences using your browser settings and, where available, our cookie banner or preference centre. Blocking some types of cookies may impact your experience of the site or limit the functionality of certain tools.

    We implement technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure, or access. Measures may include access controls, encryption in transit and at rest, network monitoring, backup strategies, staff training, and regular security assessments.

    We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this policy, including satisfying legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. Retention periods depend on the type of data, our relationship with you, and applicable legal obligations.

    When data is no longer required, we will securely delete, anonymise, or aggregate it in accordance with our data retention schedules and applicable laws.

    Depending on your jurisdiction and subject to applicable law, you may have some or all of the following rights in relation to your personal data that we process:

    • Right of access — to obtain confirmation of whether we process your personal data and to receive a copy of such data.
    • Right to rectification — to request that inaccurate or incomplete personal data be corrected or updated.
    • Right to erasure — to request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances, for example where it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected or where you have withdrawn consent.
    • Right to restriction of processing — to request that we limit the processing of your data in specific situations, such as while we verify its accuracy or our legitimate interests.
    • Right to object — to object, on grounds relating to your particular situation, to processing based on our legitimate interests, and to object at any time to the use of your data for direct marketing.
    • Right to data portability — to receive certain personal data you have provided to us in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit that data to another controller where technically feasible.
    • Rights related to consent — where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

    To exercise your rights, please use the contact options in the Contact and privacy requests section of this policy or the dedicated privacy request form linked below. We may need to verify your identity and, where applicable, confirm your relationship with a customer organisation before responding.

    Our customer base spans multiple regions, including Africa, Asia, Europe, the Americas, and Oceania. Local legal requirements may provide additional rights or obligations beyond those described generally in this policy.

    Where EU or UK data protection law applies, AgriGenomics will normally act as controller for personal data related to account management, marketing, and platform operations. For certain genomics and sequencing services, we may act as a processor on behalf of your organisation under a separate data processing agreement.

    If you are located outside the EU/EEA or UK, we will comply with the data protection laws that apply to our activities in your jurisdiction to the extent required. Local privacy notices or contract terms may supplement this policy for specific regions or services.

    If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, our data practices, or if you wish to exercise your data protection rights, you can contact our privacy team using the contact options provided below. Please include sufficient information to help us identify you and your relationship with AgriGenomics (for example, your organisation and relevant products or services).

    You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe that our processing of your personal data infringes applicable law. We encourage you to contact us first so that we can address your concerns directly.

    For the most efficient handling of requests, we recommend using our dedicated privacy request form or the email link provided in the contact options at the end of this page.
