Privacy Policy

This page explains, in practical terms, how information related to your use of pregnancy test tools, reminders, saved items, and community features on this site is handled.

You can review this policy at any time and adjust cookies and analytics preferences in the “Cookies & Analytics” section.

Jump to a section

This privacy policy explains how information is handled when you browse this site, use tools such as the When to Test Calculator, Symptom Checker, reminders and testing plans, save products or articles, or participate in the Community Q&A.

The policy focuses on three main ideas:

  • Keeping the information you share as limited and proportionate as possible.
  • Using data primarily to provide, improve, and secure pregnancy test advice and tools.
  • Giving you clear choices about cookies, analytics, saved items, and how to contact the site about your data.

References to “this site” describe how the website and its tools are designed to behave. The exact legal controller and contact route may depend on how the site is deployed; the Contact & Data Requests section explains how to reach the team responsible for your version of the site.

The site is designed to collect only the information needed to provide its tools and content. Depending on how you use the site, information may include:

Browsing and device information

  • Basic technical data such as browser type, device type, operating system, and general region (for example, country or city-level location inferred from your IP address, if enabled).
  • Pages viewed, buttons clicked, and time spent on different parts of the site (when analytics cookies are enabled).

Information you enter into tools

  • When to Test Calculator and Planner – cycle dates (for example, first day of last period), average cycle length, reasons for testing (such as trying_to_conceive or missed_period from the calculator options), and any notes you choose to enter in testing plans.
  • Reminders – reminder labels (for example, “Test with first morning urine”), reminder_datetime and timezone from your plan, and reminder status (pending, sent, missed, or done).
  • Symptom Checker – information you choose to share about late_days, nausea_level, breast_tenderness_level, other_symptoms, and the recommended_action and recommended_test_timing suggested by the checker.

Saved and bookmarked items

  • Favorite products – references to Product.id values for any pregnancy tests you mark as favorites (stored as FavoriteProduct records with favorited_at timestamps).
  • Bookmarked articles – references to Article.id values for articles you choose to save (stored as BookmarkedArticle records with saved_at timestamps).

Community Q&A content

  • Questions you submit, including title and body text, the selected category (such as test_results or general_support), timestamps like created_at, and whether the post is marked as is_anonymous.
  • Answers you provide to other users’ questions, including answer body text and timestamps.

Store locator and product browsing

  • Search terms and filters used when browsing products (for example, specific type values such as digital or strip, price ranges, or early_detection preferences).
  • Location-based searches in the store locator, such as city, state, or zip_code, and the distance range selected to find stores that carry particular pregnancy tests.

Some data can be associated with temporary identifiers (for example, an internal session ID) to allow features like comparisons or ongoing symptom checker sessions. The site is not designed to collect government ID numbers or detailed medical records.

Information is used to provide the site’s core pregnancy testing tools, keep them secure, and understand how they are used. Typical uses include:

Providing tools, content, and features

  • Running calculators and planners to suggest recommended_test_date values and test timing guidance based on the information you enter.
  • Scheduling and displaying reminders for your testing plans so you can see what is pending, sent, missed, or done.
  • Serving personalised suggestions within the site, such as related Article items or products that might be relevant to your current question (when personalization is enabled).

Analytics and improvement

  • Aggregating anonymous or pseudonymous statistics about which tools are used most, which pages are confusing, and where people stop in a flow, when analytics cookies are enabled.
  • Testing changes to the site to ensure that calculators, symptom checker flows, and community features remain understandable and helpful.

Safety, moderation, and misuse prevention

  • Reviewing Community Q&A content for safety, accuracy, and compliance with community guidelines, including detecting abusive or inappropriate posts.
  • Protecting the site from technical abuse (for example, denial-of-service attacks or automated scraping).

Legal and compliance reasons

  • Responding to legally valid requests required by applicable law, where relevant to the version of the site you are using.
  • Documenting user consent choices regarding cookies, analytics, and personalization, where such consent is legally required.

Information is not used to make automated decisions that have legal or similarly significant effects about you without meaningful human review. The site does not sell lists of users or their symptom details to third parties.

Different types of information are kept for different lengths of time, depending on how they are used:

  • Short-lived tool inputs – inputs to calculators and one-off tools (such as a single Symptom Checker session) may exist only in memory or be stored for a short period to allow you to review your results.
  • Plans and reminders – TestingPlan and Reminder records are generally kept as long as the plan remains active and for a limited time afterwards so you can review your history or mark reminders as done.
  • Saved items – FavoriteProduct and BookmarkedArticle entries are kept until you remove them or until the site clears inactive data according to its housekeeping rules.
  • Community content – QnaQuestion and QnaAnswer entries may be stored for as long as they remain relevant to the community, even if your account or identifiers change, so that previous conversations remain intact.
  • Analytics logs – aggregated analytics data is normally stored for a limited period determined by the analytics tool in use, after which it is deleted or irreversibly anonymised.

The exact retention periods may vary depending on the hosting organisation, legal requirements, and how the site is configured in your region. You can request more detail, or ask for specific information to be removed or anonymised, through the Contact & Data Requests section.

Cookies are small text files that can be stored on your device to remember information between visits. This site aims to use only a limited set of cookies, grouped into the categories below. Some cookies are essential for the site to work; others are optional and controlled by your preferences.

Types of cookies used

  • Essential cookies – required for basic site functions such as remembering that you are in the middle of a calculator flow, keeping track of temporary comparison sets (ProductComparison), or preventing abuse. These cannot usually be switched off via the preferences on this page.
  • Functional cookies – used to remember choices such as your preferred view mode in the planner (for example, week or month), language, or whether you have dismissed certain notices.
  • Analytics cookies – help measure how the site is used (for example, which tools are most popular, which articles are most read, or how often people return). These cookies are optional in many regions.
  • Personalization cookies – may be used to tailor which articles, products, or community topics are suggested to you based on previous site activity. These cookies are optional and used only when enabled.

How different tools use data

  • When you create a testing plan, the site stores a TestingPlan record with fields such as name (for example, “When to test plan”), source (calculator_when_to_test or planner_manual), recommended_test_date, and notes you choose to add. Individual Reminder entries store reminder_datetime, timezone (if available), and status (pending, sent, missed, or done).

    These details are used solely to show your plan, schedule reminders, and help you keep track of which tests you have taken. They are not intended to be shared publicly. Reminders may be removed automatically after a period of inactivity or when you delete or complete your plan.

  • SymptomCheckerSession entries may store information about your session (for example, late_days, nausea_level, breast_tenderness_level, other_symptoms, and the recommended_action such as test_now or wait_few_days). This information is used to generate on-screen guidance and may be combined with recommended_article_ids to suggest further reading.

    Session data is not intended for long-term storage and may be deleted or anonymised after a limited period, particularly if it is only needed to show your results during a single visit.

  • When you mark a pregnancy test as a favourite, the site stores a FavoriteProduct record linking your internal user or session identifier to a Product.id, along with favorited_at. When you bookmark an article, a similar BookmarkedArticle record links you to an Article.id with a saved_at timestamp.

    Saved items exist to make it easier for you to re-find tests and articles you care about. They can also be used, when personalization is enabled, to suggest related articles and tools. You can usually remove saved items individually from the Saved page or clear all saved data subject to technical limits.

  • When you post a question or answer, the site stores the text you provide (title and body), the category (such as test_results or general_support), timestamps, and whether the post is flagged as is_anonymous. Community content is generally visible to other users while it remains in published status.

    Moderators may review and, if needed, edit or remove content that violates guidelines or presents safety concerns. Archived content may be stored for audit and safety reasons even after it is no longer publicly visible.

  • The store locator may handle Store and StoreInventory records to show pharmacies and retailers near a location you search for. Typical data includes store names, addresses, product availability_status values such as in_stock or out_of_stock, and approximate distance_miles from your search location.

    Search queries may be logged in aggregate (for example, to see which areas have the most interest in a given product) but are not intended to be used to track individual movements.

The planner and reminder tools are designed to help you schedule pregnancy tests around your cycle or treatment plan. When you use them, the site may store:

  • Basic plan details such as name, source (calculator_when_to_test or planner_manual), created_at, and status (active, completed, or cancelled).
  • Cycle-related information such as last_period_date and cycle_length_days when you choose to provide them, and reason_for_testing (for example, trying_to_conceive or missed_period).
  • One or more Reminder entries, each with a label, reminder_datetime, timezone (where available), and reminder status.

This information is used to display your plan, generate to-do style lists of upcoming tests, and, where available, trigger notifications according to your preferences. Reminders are not designed to replace professional medical advice or emergency care.

You can usually edit or delete individual plans and reminders from the Planner page. When removed, they are either deleted or stored only in an anonymised, aggregated form for analytics and planning improvements.

The Symptom Checker helps you decide if and when to take a pregnancy test. To do this, it may collect and temporarily store data in SymptomCheckerSession records, including:

  • Timing information such as started_at, completed_at, and late_days.
  • Self-reported symptoms such as nausea_level, breast_tenderness_level, and other_symptoms you choose to describe.
  • Resulting fields such as recommended_action (for example, test_now, wait_few_days, no_test_needed, or see_doctor) and recommended_test_timing, plus recommended_article_ids suggesting relevant reading.

Symptom checker data is used to generate on-screen advice and may also be aggregated in de-identified form to understand which questions are most common and improve explanations. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, or monitor any medical condition and does not substitute for professional medical care.

The site allows you to save favourite pregnancy test products and bookmark articles for later. When you do this, it typically stores:

  • For favourite products – a FavoriteProduct entry linking your internal user or session identifier to a Product.id, along with favorited_at.
  • For bookmarked articles – a BookmarkedArticle entry linking to an Article.id with a saved_at timestamp.

Saved items are used to populate the Saved page and can be used, when personalization cookies are enabled, to show more relevant suggestions. You can usually remove saved items from the Saved page. Removing a saved item deletes or deactivates the corresponding record, though aggregated statistics about overall popularity may remain.

The Community Q&A feature allows people to ask and answer questions about pregnancy tests and related topics. When you participate, the site typically stores:

  • Your question text (title and body) and the category you choose, such as test_results, test_timing, ovulation_and_fertility, product_questions, or general_support.
  • Metadata such as created_at, last_activity_at, num_answers (where available), and status (submitted, published, or archived).
  • Whether your question or answer is posted as is_anonymous, which affects how it is displayed to other users.

Community posts are visible to others while published. Moderators may edit or remove posts that violate guidelines or contain unsafe advice. Even when removed from public view, some content may be kept in an archived state for moderation records, abuse prevention, or legal reasons.

To protect your privacy, you should avoid posting information that directly identifies you, such as your full name, address, or medical record numbers. If you think you have shared more than you intended, you can request that specific content be reviewed via the Contact & Data Requests section.

Your privacy rights at a glance

Depending on your location and the organisation operating this site, you may have rights over your personal information, such as the ability to see, correct, or delete data and to control how cookies and analytics are used.

  • Request an overview of the information this site holds in connection with your use of tools, reminders, saved items, or community content, where technically possible.
  • Ask for inaccurate or outdated information to be corrected, or update your saved plans and preferences directly in the tools.
  • Request deletion of certain information (for example, saved items or specific community posts) or limit how analytics and personalization are used.
  • Contact the site if you have questions about this policy, how your data is processed, or how to exercise your rights.

The exact rights you have depend on the privacy laws that apply in your region and the organisation operating this site. However, the site is designed to support the following types of rights where feasible:

Right of access

You may be able to request a summary of the information connected to your use of the site, such as records of testing plans, reminders, symptom checker sessions, saved products or articles, and community contributions, to the extent that information can be linked back to you.

Right to rectification

If any information is inaccurate or incomplete, you can ask for it to be corrected. In many cases, you can update data directly by editing or deleting plans, reminders, or saved items within the tools themselves.

Right to erasure (where applicable)

In some circumstances, you may ask for certain information to be deleted. Examples may include clearing saved plans, removing saved products or bookmarked articles, or requesting that particular community posts be taken down. Some information may still need to be retained for legal, safety, or technical reasons, but this will be limited to what is necessary.

Right to restrict or object to processing

You can adjust optional analytics and personalization settings using the Cookies & Analytics section. You may also be able to object to certain uses of your data, such as specific types of profiling, where these occur.

Right to portability (where applicable)

In some regions, you may have the right to receive certain information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, for example to take your testing plan data elsewhere. If this applies, the site team can explain what formats are available.

Right to complain to a supervisory authority

If you are not satisfied with how your information or privacy requests are handled, you may have the right to lodge a complaint with the data protection or privacy authority in your country or region. The Contact & Data Requests section explains how to reach the site team first, which is often the quickest way to resolve issues.

To exercise these rights, use the options provided in each tool (for example, deleting a plan or unsaving an article) or submit a request using the form in the Contact & Data Requests section. Please include enough detail to help locate the relevant data (for example, the approximate dates of your usage or links to specific community posts).

Contact & Data Requests

If you have questions about this privacy policy, how your information is handled, or if you want to exercise your privacy rights, you can use the form below. Your message will be passed to the team responsible for operating this site or the organisation that provided it.

Do not include urgent medical questions or emergencies in this form. For medical concerns, contact a healthcare professional or local emergency services instead.

If your request is about a specific tool, please mention which one and any relevant dates or on-screen labels (for example, the name of a testing plan or the title of a community question).
