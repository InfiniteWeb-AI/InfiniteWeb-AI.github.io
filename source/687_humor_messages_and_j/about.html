About Humor Messages & Jokes

How this site picks jokes, tailors recommendations, and keeps things comfortable for different audiences.

Mission

A calm, curated place for jokes and humor messages

Humor Messages & Jokes is designed as a clean reading space for short-form humor. Instead of chasing constant notifications, we focus on well-formatted jokes you can skim, save, and reuse when you need a quick laugh or the perfect punchline.

You will find a mix of joke styles, all stored as Joke records in our system:

  • Quick one-liners and wordplay puns
  • Classic Dad Jokes and groan-worthy setups
  • Knock-knock routines
  • Short situational stories and conversation-style jokes

Behind the scenes, every joke has structured fields like categoryId, contentRating (clean or nsfw), language, lengthChars, and optional tags. These fields are used to power filters, preferences, and recommendations on other pages such as Home, Browse, Categories, and Random.

Humor categories

Jokes are grouped into Category entries such as Dad Jokes, Puns, Knock-Knock, and One-liners. Each category keeps track of basic stats like jokeCount and, in some cases, an overall average rating. When you browse categories, you are really exploring different slices of the same underlying Joke collection.

Preferences

Content preferences, maturity, and length controls

The Settings page lets you configure a single ContentPreference profile for your account. These settings directly affect what you see on the homepage and in recommended lists.

What your ContentPreference controls

  • cleanOnly — When set to true, any Joke with contentRating = "nsfw" is hidden from your personalized feeds (Home, most recommendations) and de-emphasized elsewhere.
  • preferredCategoryIds — A list of category IDs (for example, ["one_liners","puns"]) that should show up more often and higher in your homepage feed.
  • blockedCategoryIds — Categories you do not want to see. Jokes in these categories are filtered out or heavily de-prioritized.
  • maxJokeLengthChars — Your preferred upper limit for joke length. We compare this to each Joke's lengthChars so you can keep your feed focused on shorter or longer reads.
  • homepageSortOrder — How your homepage is ordered: recommended, top_rated, most_recent, or most_shared. This controls which Joke fields (like rating, postedAt, or shareCount) we prioritize in that context.

These settings do not change the jokes themselves. They simply tell the site how to sort, filter, and prioritize existing Joke records for you. The same Joke may appear very differently for someone with different ContentPreference values.

Policies

Policies, safety, and getting help

Humor can be subjective, and we aim to keep this space respectful and safe. When a joke crosses the line or simply is not for you, there are tools and policies in place to handle it.

Reporting jokes

On joke pages and feeds you may see a Report or similar action. Submitting a report creates a JokeReport entry with a structured reason such as offensive, nsfw, spam, or duplicate, plus an optional free-text comment.

Our moderation tools review these JokeReport records and update their status as open, reviewed, resolved, or dismissed. If a report is upheld, the underlying Joke record may be edited, re-tagged, or removed from public feeds.

Recommendations

How recommendations work across the site

When you see jokes on the homepage or in Recommended for you sections, the ordering is based on a mix of the joke's own properties and your past actions. Below is a simplified description of how we use different entities from our data model.

Joke quality signals

Each Joke carries its own popularity and quality metrics:

  • rating — The average star rating (0–5) from all users. Higher-rated jokes are more likely to surface when your homepageSortOrder is recommended or top_rated.
  • ratingCount — How many people have rated the joke. This helps us avoid showing a freshly posted but barely-rated joke as a top result.
  • shareCount — How often a joke has been shared. This is used for Most shared sorts and to lightly boost especially shareable items.
  • postedAt — The date a joke was published. This drives most_recent sorting and fresh-content sections.
  • contentRating & lengthChars — Used together with your ContentPreference to filter out NSFW jokes when cleanOnly = true, and to respect your preferred maximum length.

In recommendation contexts, a joke with a slightly lower rating but many more ratings can be prioritized over one with a perfect 5.0 but very few votes. This prevents rating spikes from small samples and keeps widely-liked jokes visible.

Your actions and how they shape recommendations

Several of your actions create specific records that help us understand what you enjoy:

  • Favorites — Marking a joke as favorite creates a Favorite entry pointing to that Joke. Favorites are easy to resurface on the Favorites page, and are a strong signal when building your Recommended for you feed.
  • Collections — When you add a joke to a collection, we create a CollectionItem (which links a Joke to a specific Collection). This helps us understand which jokes you group together (for example, an Office Party set) and can inspire similar recommendations.
  • Category follows — Following a category creates a CategoryFollow record tied to that Category. Followed categories are emphasized in your homepage and category-based recommendations.
  • Reading list — Saving a joke for later reading creates a ReadingListItem. This is a softer signal than a favorite but still indicates interest.

Content preferences in the recommendation mix

Your ContentPreference settings act as a filter and ordering guide:

  • We always respect cleanOnly when assembling personalized feeds.
  • Jokes whose categoryId appears in preferredCategoryIds are boosted, while those in blockedCategoryIds may be removed entirely.
  • maxJokeLengthChars is applied before ranking, so overly long jokes are filtered out if you prefer shorter content.
  • Finally, homepageSortOrder chooses the primary sort: recommended, top_rated, most_recent, or most_shared.

User actions

What you can do with jokes, and what those actions mean

Most of the buttons you see next to jokes map directly to structured data in our system. Here is how the most common actions work and how they relate to other pages.

Jump to:

Rating jokes

When you rate a joke, your vote contributes to that Joke's rating and ratingCount. Highly rated jokes are more likely to:

  • Appear near the top when your homepageSortOrder is top_rated.
  • Be highlighted in category views and some recommended sections.

Favorites

Clicking a Favorite or heart control creates a Favorite record pointing to that Joke. Favorites:

  • Show up on the Favorites page.
  • Act as a strong positive signal in recommendation logic.

Collections

Creating or editing a collection (for example, Office Party or Family-Friendly Set) creates a Collection. Adding jokes to it creates CollectionItem records that connect specific Joke IDs to that collection.

  • Collections make it easy to reuse jokes in a specific context.
  • They tell our system which jokes you think belong together, which can lightly influence recommendations.

Following categories

When you follow a category (such as puns or dad_jokes), we create a CategoryFollow entry with isActive = true. Followed categories:

  • Are prioritized on the homepage and other personalized feeds.
  • May be suggested more often when we propose new jokes or collections.

Reporting jokes

Using the report feature creates a JokeReport with a specific reason (such as offensive, spam, or wrong_language) and optional comment. Reports do not directly change recommendations, but they can lead to moderation actions that remove or reclassify content.

Managing content preferences

Adjusting your settings on the Settings page updates your single ContentPreference record. Changes to cleanOnly, preferredCategoryIds, blockedCategoryIds, maxJokeLengthChars, or homepageSortOrder will be reflected the next time you load the homepage or a personalized list.

For a structured explanation of how those preferences interact with ratings and favorites, see .

Reading list

When you save a joke for later, we create a ReadingListItem that links your account to that Joke and stores how it was added (for example, copied_link vs add_button). These items appear on the Reading List page for easy access.
