Privacy Policy

This page explains how Humor Messages & Jokes handles information related to your favorites, collections, content preferences, and other single-user features, and how your browser stores these settings.

1. Overview

Humor Messages & Jokes is designed as a single-user humor reading experience. Features such as Favorites, Collections, Reading List, Category Follows, and Content Preferences work only for you in your current browser and do not create a public profile or social feed.

This Privacy Policy describes what data these features rely on, how that data is stored (typically in your browser as local data like favorites or content_preferences), how long it is kept, and what controls you have.

  • We store only the information needed to provide single-user features such as saving jokes or remembering your content filters.
  • Most personalization data is stored in your browser (for example using local storage) under keys that match the data model, such as favorites or collections.
  • This privacy page itself is stateless: navigation between sections uses only URL parameters and hash fragments.
  • You can reset or clear your stored data at any time, either via browser tools or through site-level controls (for example, on the Settings page).

2. Data categories and purpose

The site uses several internal data structures to power your experience. Conceptually, these are stored under browser keys that match the data dictionary (for example, favorites, collections, content_preferences). They are used only to provide functionality for you as a single user in this browser.

2.1 Joke content

The Joke entity is the core content you browse. Joke records (fields like text, categoryId, language, contentRating, and lengthChars) describe the jokes themselves and are not personal to you. Jokes are usually pre-generated and stored on the server.

2.2 Favorites

When you mark a joke as a favorite, the site records a Favorite entry (stored under the favorites key). Each favorite typically contains:

  • jokeId – which joke you liked;
  • source – where you favorited it (for example, home or browse);
  • addedAt – when it was added.

This information lets the Favorites page show you a list of jokes you have marked, but it is not used for cross-user profiling.

2.3 Collections and collection items

You can group jokes into your own collections such as “Office Party” or “Knock Knock Set”. This uses two structures:

  • Collection (stored under collections): high-level details like name, optional description, and flags like isPinned.
  • CollectionItem (stored under collection_items): links each collection to individual jokes via collectionId and jokeId, plus optional ordering information.

These exist so you can re-open and manage curated joke sets later. They are not visible to other users through this site.

2.4 Reading list

The ReadingListItem entity (stored under reading_list_items) lets you save jokes “for later”. Each item tracks:

  • jokeId – which joke you saved;
  • source – how it was added (for example, add_button);
  • sourceUrl – the internal link used, if applicable;
  • addedAt – when it was saved.

2.5 Category follows

If you follow particular categories (for example, Dad Jokes or Puns), the site records CategoryFollow entries (stored under category_follows) with fields like:

  • categoryId – the category you follow;
  • isActive – whether the follow is currently on;
  • followedAt – when you started following.

This allows the homepage or browse views to highlight categories you care about.

2.6 Content preferences

Your viewing rules and filters are represented by the ContentPreference entity (stored under content_preferences). For example:

  • cleanOnly – whether to hide jokes with contentRating of nsfw;
  • preferredCategoryIds / blockedCategoryIds – categories you want more or less of;
  • maxJokeLengthChars – your preferred maximum joke length;
  • homepageSortOrder – how jokes should be ordered (for example, top_rated or most_recent).

These settings are used only to personalize how content is displayed to you. They do not affect how others see the site.

2.7 Joke reports and moderation

If you report a joke, the site may create a JokeReport entry (stored under joke_reports) with fields like:

  • jokeId – what you reported;
  • reason – why (for example, spam, offensive, or duplicate);
  • comment – optional free-text details;
  • source – where you reported it (for example, random_feed).

Joke reports may be sent to the site’s moderation tools and retained as needed to review, resolve, or audit content decisions. They are not used to build a behavioral profile of you.

2.8 Retention and scope

For browser-stored data such as favorites, collections, follows, and content preferences, the default retention is “until you clear it” – either through site controls or via your browser’s storage and cookie settings. For moderation data such as joke reports, entries may be kept longer for integrity and safety reasons.

For details on how these items are stored in your browser, see the section on Cookies & browser storage.

3. Cookies and browser storage

This site relies primarily on your browser’s storage mechanisms (such as local storage or similar technology) to keep track of your preferences and saved items. In some cases, small cookies may also be used for strictly necessary technical reasons (for example, to remember that you have dismissed a one-time notice).

The privacy policy for this page is intentionally URL-driven: navigation uses query parameters (like ?section=overview) and hash fragments (like #overview), rather than cookies, to remember where you are.

3.1 What may be stored in your browser

Examples of values that may be stored locally in your browser include:

  • Favorites under the favorites key, containing data about jokes you marked to keep.
  • Collections and CollectionItems under collections and collection_items, describing the joke sets you created.
  • Reading list under reading_list_items, so you can return to jokes saved for later.
  • Category follows under category_follows, indicating which categories you follow.
  • Content preferences under content_preferences, to apply your filters and sort order across sessions.
  • Non-identifying UI settings, such as last-used sort option or filter combination in the browsing interface.

3.2 How this differs from tracking cookies

The information stored in your browser is scoped to this single-user humor experience and is intended to make the site more useful to you. It is not designed to track your activity across other websites or to build advertising profiles.

URL parameters like section and mode are used solely for in-page navigation and optional print mode. They are not tied to any account or identifier and are not used to follow you elsewhere.

3.3 Clearing or resetting browser storage

You remain in control of this data. You can reset or remove stored information in several ways:

  • Use your browser’s settings to clear cookies and site data for this domain, which will remove locally stored keys like favorites and content_preferences.
  • Use in-site controls such as the Settings page to reset content preferences or clear lists (for example, removing items from Favorites or Reading List).
  • Use private/incognito browsing modes if you prefer that no local data be retained between sessions.

For a focused description of how personalization works, see Content preferences and personalization.

4. Content preferences and personalization

The site uses your ContentPreference settings to tailor which jokes are highlighted and how lists are ordered. These settings apply only to your experience on this site, in this browser.

4.1 Types of preferences

Key fields in the content_preferences structure include:

  • cleanOnly – when true, jokes whose contentRating is nsfw are hidden from areas like Home and Browse.
  • preferredCategoryIds – category IDs (for example, dad_jokes or puns) that are emphasized on the homepage or in recommendations.
  • blockedCategoryIds – category IDs you explicitly do not want to see in feeds.
  • maxJokeLengthChars – your preferred maximum joke length, used to filter out items whose lengthChars exceeds this value.
  • homepageSortOrder – your preferred ordering of jokes, such as recommended, top_rated, most_recent, or most_shared.

4.2 How preferences are applied

When you visit pages like Home or Browse, the site reads your stored content_preferences entry from your browser. It uses these values to:

  • Filter out jokes that do not meet your clean-content or length rules.
  • Prioritize categories you like while de-emphasizing or hiding blocked ones.
  • Sort jokes according to your chosen homepageSortOrder.

These decisions are made on a per-request basis to personalize your view. They do not change the underlying jokes or how the site appears for other users.

4.3 Retention and changes

Content preferences are usually kept until you change them or clear your browser storage. You can update them at any time via the Settings page or other controls provided near filters and sort options.

If you prefer not to have personalization remembered between visits, you can clear site data in your browser or use private/incognito windows so that your content_preferences entry is not retained.

5. Your privacy choices and controls

You have several options for controlling how data is stored and used to personalize this single-user site.

5.1 Managing favorites, collections, and lists

  • You can remove individual jokes from Favorites, Collections, or Reading List using controls on those pages.
  • Deleting a collection or clearing a list removes the associated entries (for example, from collections or reading_list_items) from your browser and/or the site’s storage.

5.2 Changing content preferences

Your maturity filters and topic preferences are editable at any time via the Settings page or similar controls. You can:

  • Turn cleanOnly on or off.
  • Add or remove preferred and blocked categories.
  • Adjust your maximum joke length and default sorting.

5.3 Clearing browser-stored data

For a complete reset of locally stored data related to this site, you can use your browser’s built-in tools:

  • In most browsers, you can open “Site settings” or “Privacy & security” and clear cookies and site data for this domain.
  • This will remove locally stored keys such as favorites, collections, category_follows, and content_preferences.
  • After clearing, the site will behave as if you are visiting for the first time from that browser.

5.4 Opting out of personalization

If you wish to minimize personalization:

  • Avoid turning on optional features that require stored preferences (for example, do not follow categories if you do not want category-specific feeds).
  • Disable or reset content preferences so the site uses its default filters and sort order.
  • Use private/incognito browsing if you do not want site data kept between sessions.

5.5 Questions or requests

If you have questions about how these choices work, or if something does not behave as expected, you can contact the site owner using the details in the Contact & questions section below.

6. Contact and privacy questions

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how your preferences and saved items are handled, you can reach out using the email address below.

Typical reasons to contact us include:

  • Questions about how favorites, collections, or content preferences are stored and used.
  • Concerns about a specific joke or a moderation decision related to a JokeReport.
  • Suggestions for improving privacy controls or transparency.

Email: privacy@humor-messages.example

When you contact us, please avoid including sensitive personal information in your message. Describe your request in general terms (for example, which browser and device you use, which page you were on, and what you expected to happen).
