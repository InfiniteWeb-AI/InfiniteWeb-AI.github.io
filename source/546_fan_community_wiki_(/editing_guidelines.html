1. Purpose of these guidelines

This page is an Article.type = guidelines document for the film community wiki. It describes how to create and maintain film articles, curated lists, templates, redirects/merges, trivia sections, references, Characters sections, See also links, and user profile conventions.

The text on this page is stored in the Article data model and rendered here by the guidelines article loader. Section navigation, examples, and other tools read the structure of this article rather than hardcoding section names.

2. Creating film pages

2.1 Title format

Film pages use Article.type = film. Titles normally follow the pattern Base title (YEAR film), for example The Midnight Signal (2022 film). This avoids collisions when multiple films share the same base title.

  • Include the release year in parentheses for most films.
  • If there are multiple films from the same year with the same title, use a qualifier such as country or genre in the parentheses.
  • Do not include actor names, taglines, or marketing slogans in titles.

2.2 Lead summary

The summary field on the Article holds the lead section. It should be a concise 1–3 sentence overview: title, year, country, main genre, and a brief premise. Avoid plot spoilers in the lead.

2.3 Standard sections

A typical film article body (Article.content) is organized into predictable sections so readers know where to look for details:

  • Plot – A neutral, spoiler-aware summary of the story. Long films may use subsections if needed.
  • Cast – Structured list of actors and roles, based on FilmCredit data, usually ordered by billing.
  • Characters – Focused descriptions of major characters, their arcs, and key relationships. Minor roles can be covered briefly here instead of individual pages.
  • Production – Optional section on writing, direction, filming locations, and behind-the-scenes context.
  • Reception – Summaries of reviews, awards, and notable audience responses.
  • Trivia – Optional, carefully curated facts (see the trivia section below).
  • See also – Internal links to related films, lists, or topics using InternalLink.context = see_also.
  • References – Citations to sources that support the article.

2.4 Characters and minor roles

Not every character needs a dedicated Article.type = character page. For most minor roles, the film's Characters section is the right place:

  • Create separate character articles only for characters with substantial narrative importance and enough sourced information to justify their own page.
  • When a previously separate character article is merged back into the film page, set the character article's Article.isRedirect = true and redirectTargetArticleId to the film's article id.
  • In the film's Characters section, include a short paragraph summarizing the merged character's role so the redirect remains meaningful.

2.5 Trivia and See also on film pages

Use the film's Trivia section sparingly. Trivia should be sourced, notable, and not just restate plot or promotional material. Good candidates include production anecdotes, running jokes, or creator-confirmed references to other works.

The See also section should contain a small number of internal links to related articles, represented as InternalLink rows with InternalLink.context = see_also. Prefer linking to:

  • Other films in the same narrative universe.
  • Curated lists that feature this film (e.g. a "1999 Hidden Gems" list).
  • Relevant templates, if they have their own documentation article.

2.6 References and sourcing for films

All controversial or detailed claims (box office, critical reception, production stories) should be backed by references stored in the References section. Provide enough information so readers can locate the source (outlet, author, date, and a link when possible).

2.7 Categories for film pages

Film articles are organized using ArticleCategory records that link each film article to Category rows. Film-related categories belong to the group Category.group = films.

  • Every film article should be in at least one year-based category (e.g. 2022 films).
  • Add genre-based categories (e.g. "Mystery thriller films") and country categories when known.
  • Do not create extremely narrow one-off categories (such as a category just for a single film) unless there is a clear need.

3. List pages

Curated lists use Article.type = list. They can be year-based (e.g. 1999 Hidden Gems), genre-based, or themed ("Films set on trains"). Lists are not meant to be complete databases; they are curated viewpoints that must still be sourced and neutral.

3.1 Naming and scope

  • Choose a concise, descriptive title that accurately matches the selection criteria.
  • Explain the inclusion criteria in a short introductory paragraph (release years, genres, regions, or critical thresholds).
  • Make clear whether the list is exhaustive (attempts to include every qualifying film) or curated (a selection based on specific sources or community consensus).

3.2 List item structure and InternalLink context

Each list item should link to the film's article where possible. These links are tracked as InternalLink rows, usually with InternalLink.context = list_item.

  • Use a bullet list or numbered list for items; avoid one-item-per-paragraph formatting.
  • Each item should at least contain the film title and year; optionally add a short 1–2 sentence note explaining its relevance to the list theme.
  • When a film does not yet have an article, you may include a redlink-style placeholder, but avoid long stretches of unsourced speculation in the description.

3.3 Sourcing for lists

Curated lists should rely on verifiable criteria rather than personal taste alone. Some acceptable bases for inclusion:

  • An external critic's list or poll that is cited in the References section.
  • Aggregated ratings (e.g., films with Film.userRatingAverage >= 4.0) when this is clearly stated.
  • Community votes summarized on the associated talk page or poll, with transparent counts.

Each list article should still include a References section documenting any important claims about the films or the list's selection criteria.

4. Templates, navigation boxes, merges and redirects

Reusable layout and navigation elements live in articles with Article.type = template. Redirects allow older or duplicate pages (especially minor characters) to point cleanly to a better target article.

4.1 Template articles (Article.type = template)

Templates are small, reusable snippets of markup that can be inserted into multiple articles, such as navigation boxes for trilogies, franchises, or recurring directors. They are stored as regular Article rows with Article.type = template.

  • A template article's title usually makes the purpose clear (for example, including the word "Template" or another convention agreed by the community).
  • When a film article uses a navigation box template, the relationship is recorded conceptually as an InternalLink with InternalLink.context = template_usage.
  • Links inside the navigation box (to films, characters, or lists) are represented as InternalLink rows with InternalLink.context = navigation_template_link so they can be analyzed separately from in-text links.
  • Avoid over-templating. If a layout is only used on one article, a normal section is usually better than a template.

4.2 Redirects and merges

Redirects are Articles where Article.isRedirect = true and redirectTargetArticleId points to the canonical target. They are used when two titles should lead to the same content or when a topic has been merged into a broader article.

  • After merging a minor character page into the film's Characters section, keep the old character article but mark it as a redirect to the film's article so incoming links and bookmarks continue to work.
  • Use redirects for alternate spellings, nicknames, or working titles of films when these are verifiable and likely search terms.
  • Do not set Article.isRedirect = true on pages that still contain substantial content; move the content first, then convert the source into a redirect.

When editing redirects, keep the edit summary clear (for example, "Merged minor character into film Characters section; converted page to redirect"). The underlying redirect fields are handled by the editor and backend; editors mainly decide when redirects and merges are appropriate.

5. Trivia sections

Trivia sections can add value when they highlight genuinely interesting, sourced information that does not fit cleanly into Plot, Production, or Reception. They should not become dumping grounds for fan theories or unsourced speculation.

  • Each trivia item should be concise and verifiable. Provide a reference when the fact is not obvious from watching the film.
  • Avoid listing every continuity error or easter egg; prioritize items discussed in reliable sources.
  • Do not include personal reactions ("This is the scariest scene") or instructions to the reader ("You must watch this twice to notice...").

6. References and sourcing

Every article should support key claims with references stored as part of Article.content. While the exact citation markup may vary, the goals are consistent: verifiability, clarity, and enough information for readers to locate the source.

  • Use references for release dates, box office figures, awards, and direct quotations.
  • Summaries of critical reception should be based on reviews or aggregators, not personal opinion.
  • For curated lists, provide at least one reference explaining the selection criteria or the external list being summarized.

7. Characters, people pages, and profiles

Characters and people are represented in multiple ways:

  • Article.type = character for major fictional characters with enough independent coverage.
  • Article.type = user_profile for user profile pages that may contain favorite performances, watchlists, and personal notes.
  • Person and FilmCredit rows for structured data about actors and crew.

When deciding whether to create a standalone character article or keep details on the film page:

  • If the character appears in multiple films or has substantial coverage in reliable sources, a dedicated character article is appropriate.
  • If information about the character is limited to basic plot description, keep details in the film's Characters section instead.
  • When merging a character back into the film, convert the character article into a redirect using Article.isRedirect = true and redirectTargetArticleId as described above.

7.1 User profile and list style

User profile pages (Article.type = user_profile) often include sections like Favorite Performances, Top 10 Horror Films, or yearly watchlogs. These sections should still follow basic wiki style and linking conventions.

  • When linking to a film or actor from a profile, the link is represented as an InternalLink with InternalLink.context = favorite_performance (for performances) or InternalLink.context = list_item (for general lists).
  • Keep descriptions short and avoid heavy spoilers; profiles are informal but should still be considerate of other readers.
  • Do not present personal ratings or opinions as facts. It is fine to say "I found this performance understated" but not "This is objectively the actor's best role".
  • Profiles are not a substitute for film or character articles. Substantial production or plot information belongs on those dedicated pages, not only on user profiles.

8. See also sections and internal links

See also sections help readers discover related articles. They also feed structured link data via InternalLink rows with InternalLink.context = see_also. Keep these sections focused and relevant.

  • Prefer links to related films, list pages, or templates that genuinely add context.
  • Avoid long, unsorted link dumps; 3–10 high-quality links are usually enough.
  • Where possible, ensure each See also link is reciprocated (the target article also links back in its See also or appropriate section).

Other internal links (for example, from within Plot or Trivia) are usually stored with InternalLink.context = other unless they match a specific context like list_item, favorite_performance, or navigation_template_link.

9. Categories and organization

Categories group related articles to make browsing easier. They are modeled by the Category entity, linked to articles via ArticleCategory. Film-related categories have Category.group = films and are exposed on the categories page using category.html?categoryId=films.

  • Assign each film to at least one year and one genre category.
  • Assign list articles to categories that describe their scope (for example, a list of 1990s thrillers might belong in both a year and a genre category).
  • Template and guidelines articles may also live in film-related categories if they describe film topics, but they should not be mixed into categories meant only for films.

Use the View film categories link in the sidebar to see how categories are structured and to avoid inventing near-duplicates of existing categories.
