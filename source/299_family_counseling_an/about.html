Our mission & approach

FamilyCenter provides accessible family counseling and relationship education for couples, parents, and caregivers. We combine evidence-informed practices with practical tools that can be used at home between sessions.

We recognize that every family system is unique. Our counselors and educators draw from approaches such as emotionally focused therapy, cognitive-behavioral strategies, attachment-informed parenting, and strengths-based coaching. Sessions and learning materials are designed to be respectful of culture, faith, and family structure.

Mission: To help families build safer, kinder, and more connected relationships by offering trustworthy guidance, skills-based education, and supportive spaces to practice new patterns together.

Core values

  • Care with dignity. We honor each person's story and pace, creating space where all family members can be heard.
  • Practical tools. Every session or course aims to leave you with clear next steps you can try at home.
  • Whole-family focus. We pay attention to individual needs while also supporting the health of the overall family system.
  • Collaboration. We work alongside you and, when appropriate, your existing supports such as medical providers, schools, or community leaders.

How counseling and education fit together

Some families come to FamilyCenter primarily for counseling, others for structured classes and self-paced courses, and many combine both. Our platform makes it easy to move between options: you might begin with a webinar on managing conflict, complete a self-assessment to understand patterns, and then schedule a focused counseling series with a counselor who specializes in your family's needs.

Our counseling & education team

FamilyCenter partners with licensed counselors, therapists, and educators who bring both clinical training and real-world family experience. Below is a sample of the roles represented on our team.

Licensed marriage & family therapists

Support couples, parents, and families working through communication challenges, conflict cycles, and life transitions such as blending families or welcoming a new child.

Many of our LMFTs have advanced training in emotionally focused couples therapy, Gottman Method interventions, and family systems work for concerns like sibling conflict or intergenerational stress.

Professional counselors & social workers

Offer individual and family counseling for concerns such as anxiety, parenting stress, anger management, and co-parenting after separation or divorce.

Our counselors draw on cognitive-behavioral, trauma-informed, and strengths-based approaches, and collaborate with community resources when helpful.

Parenting & relationship educators

Design workshops, webinars, and multi-week programs focused on practical skills: emotion coaching, calm discipline, conflict resolution, and building connection in busy seasons.

Education programs are structured around clear learning goals, take-home practices, and optional assessments so you can track growth over time.

To explore specific counselors, review credentials, and check availability, visit the Find Counselors page. For education offerings such as webinars and courses, see Workshops & Webinars and Programs & Courses.
