Privacy Policy

We take your privacy seriously. This Privacy Policy describes how FamilyCenter collects, uses, and protects information when you browse our site, book counseling sessions, register for workshops or programs, or otherwise interact with our services.

This policy applies to information collected through this website and related online tools only. It does not replace any privacy disclosures you may receive directly from individual counselors, who may have additional professional or regulatory requirements.

Information we collect

We collect information in three main ways:

  • Information you provide directly: When you complete forms (for example, to request information, book a session, register for a webinar, or enroll in a program), we may collect your name, contact details, preferred contact method, basic family details you choose to share, and any notes you provide.
  • Usage information: We collect limited technical information about how you use our site, such as pages visited, approximate location (based on IP address), device type, and browser type. This helps us understand which resources are most useful.
  • Transaction information: If you purchase a paid program or webinar, we collect information about the program or webinar selected and the transaction amount. Payment card details are processed by our payment provider and are not stored on this site.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive health information through this website—clinical information is typically shared directly with your counselor during sessions, not through online forms.

Cookies & similar technologies

Our site uses a small number of cookies and similar technologies to make the website work as expected and to understand basic usage patterns.

  • Essential cookies: These are needed for core functionality, such as maintaining a session while you move between pages or keeping items in your cart.
  • Performance and analytics cookies: These help us understand how many visitors use the site and which pages are most helpful, so we can improve our content and navigation.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling some cookies may affect how the site functions.

How we share information

We do not sell your personal information. We share information only in limited circumstances:

  • With service providers: We may share information with trusted vendors who help us operate the website (for example, hosting, payment processing, or email delivery). These providers are only permitted to use your information to perform services on our behalf.
  • With counselors and facilitators: When you book a counseling session, register for a webinar, or enroll in a program, we share relevant registration details with the counselor or facilitator so they can provide the service.
  • For legal or safety reasons: We may disclose information if required by law or when we believe it is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.

If you have questions about how a particular counselor uses or stores your information, please ask them directly; their professional obligations may go beyond the scope of this website policy.

Data retention & security

We retain information only for as long as it is reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, or as required by law. For example, records of program enrollments and webinar registrations may be kept for administrative, accounting, and audit purposes.

We use appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect your information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. No system can be guaranteed to be 100% secure, but we regularly review our safeguards.

Your choices & rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have rights regarding your personal information, such as the ability to request access, correction, or deletion. You can also ask us to limit certain uses of your information.

To make a request, please contact us using the contact details on the Contact section of this site. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling your request.

You can opt out of non-essential email updates at any time by using the unsubscribe link included in those messages. Transactional or service-related emails (for example, confirmations or reminders about your bookings) are part of our service and may not offer an unsubscribe option.

For more information about how your behavior on the site should align with our values, please also review our Online Conduct & Content Usage Policy.

Cancellation & Rescheduling Policy

We understand that family schedules can change. These guidelines explain how we handle cancellations, rescheduling, and no-shows for counseling sessions, workshops and webinars, and structured programs.

Counseling sessions

When you book a counseling session, your counselor sets aside that time for you. To respect their schedule and other clients:

  • Rescheduling: You may typically reschedule a session up to 24 hours before the scheduled start time, subject to the counselor's availability.
  • Cancellations: Cancellations made at least 24 hours before the session start time are usually eligible for a credit or refund, as described at the time of booking.
  • Late cancellations / no-shows: If you cancel with less than 24 hours' notice, or do not attend a scheduled session, the session fee may be charged in full at the counselor's discretion.

Specific counselors may have stricter or more flexible policies. Any counselor-specific rules communicated during booking will take priority over this general summary.

Workshops & webinars

For group events such as workshops and webinars:

  • Registration changes: You may be able to transfer your registration to another date or event when available. Transfer options vary by event.
  • Refunds: Unless otherwise noted on the event page, cancellations made at least 72 hours before the scheduled start time may be eligible for a refund or credit. Cancellations with less notice may not be refundable, due to limited group capacity.
  • Event changes by organizers: If an event is postponed or canceled by the organizer, we will offer a rescheduled date, a credit, or a refund, as appropriate.

Programs & courses

Structured programs and courses may involve multiple sessions, materials, or cohort-based schedules. Because of this, their cancellation and refund terms may differ from single-session bookings.

  • Enrollment changes: Some programs allow you to defer your start date to a later cohort or move to a self-paced option when available.
  • Refund windows: Many programs offer a limited refund window (for example, until after the first group session). After that point, fees may become non-refundable.
  • Non-transferable benefits: Access to digital resources, downloads, or assessments included with a program may not be returnable once accessed.

Program-specific terms will be shown during the enrollment process. Where program terms differ from this general policy, the program-specific terms will apply.

How to request changes

To request a cancellation or reschedule:

  • Use the instructions or links provided in your confirmation email, where available.
  • Or contact us using the details in the Contact section, including your name, the service booked, and the original date and time.

Requests are considered received when we confirm them or when processed through an automated link or portal. We aim to respond as quickly as possible, but response times may vary.

Online Conduct & Content Usage Policy

FamilyCenter is designed to be a supportive, respectful environment for families, couples, and individuals. These guidelines apply to your use of our website, online groups, chat or comment features (when available), and any educational content you access through this site.

Respectful communication

We ask all participants to:

  • Communicate with kindness, even when discussing difficult topics.
  • Listen to and respect different lived experiences, cultures, and family structures.
  • Avoid language that is harassing, hateful, threatening, or demeaning.

Prohibited activities

You agree not to use this site or any related communication channels to:

  • Engage in harassment, bullying, hate speech, or discrimination.
  • Share content that is unlawful, abusive, defamatory, obscene, or otherwise inappropriate.
  • Share others' confidential or identifying information without their explicit permission.
  • Promote or advertise products or services unrelated to the counseling or educational context of this site.
  • Attempt to gain unauthorized access to our systems or other users' information.

Content usage & intellectual property

All educational materials, articles, videos, assessments, worksheets, and other resources available on this site are protected by copyright and other intellectual property rights.

  • You may use resources for your personal or family learning and growth.
  • You may not redistribute, republish, or commercially exploit our content without prior written permission.
  • If you are a professional wishing to use these materials in your work, please contact us to discuss licensing options.

For details about how we handle your personal information in connection with these resources, please see our Privacy Policy.

Community & online group guidelines

If you participate in online groups, webinars, or discussion spaces associated with our programs:

  • Honor confidentiality. Do not share stories or details that could identify other participants.
  • Share from your own experience, and avoid giving unqualified professional advice to others.
  • Bring safety concerns or boundary issues to the facilitator's attention promptly.

Reporting concerns

If you experience or witness behavior that appears to violate this policy, or if you have concerns about safety or respect in any of our online spaces, please let us know as soon as possible using the contact information on the Contact page.

We may take a range of actions in response to violations, which can include reminders, removal of content, limiting access to specific features, or in some cases removing access to programs or groups.
