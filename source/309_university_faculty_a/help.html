Using the faculty profiles site

Faculty search and directory

The Faculty Directory lists all faculty records and lets you search by name, department, research keywords, and teaching status. Use this when you need to identify potential advisors, collaborators, or instructors.

Start with a broad search, then refine your results using filters such as primary department, graduate faculty status, or whether a faculty member is currently teaching this semester.

Quick steps

  1. Open the Faculty Directory using the link in the main navigation or below.
  2. Search by name or keyword to narrow the list.
  3. Use filters to focus on specific departments, ranks, or teaching status.
  4. Open an individual profile to see courses, publications, office hours, and more.

To see these options in context, open the Faculty Directory in a new tab.

Additional tips

    Advanced filters for faculty search

    Advanced filters let you move beyond simple name or department lookups. They expose research, teaching, and appointment attributes so you can find faculty who match specific academic interests or constraints.

    • Research keywords: search for topics such as “machine learning” or “memory” across faculty research profiles.
    • Joint appointments: focus on faculty with roles in more than one department using the joint_appointment designation.
    • Teaching status: filter by currently_teaching_this_semester, not_currently_teaching, or on_leave when planning courses or thesis advising.
    • Graduate faculty: limit results to faculty who are eligible to supervise graduate research using the is_graduate_faculty flag.

    When viewing a specific term’s courses, you can also filter by course-level attributes such as term, level (undergraduate vs. graduate), meeting days, and time windows.

    To experiment with these options, open the Faculty Directory with filters .

    Saved lists & planning tools

    The site provides several personal lists to help you track publications, courses, and potential thesis advisors. These lists do not affect anyone else’s view; they are only visible in your browser.

    Reading List

    On a faculty member’s Publications tab, use the “Save to Reading List” controls to collect articles, chapters, or books you want to revisit.

    Open the Reading List page to skim titles, follow links, or remove items you no longer need.

    Planned Courses

    When browsing course offerings for a faculty member or department, add courses to your personal plan using “Add to Planned Courses”.

    Visit the Planned Courses page to check for time conflicts, credit loads, and term-by-term balance.

    Thesis Advisor Shortlist

    Use “Add to Thesis Advisor Shortlist” on faculty profiles to build a small set of prospective advisors who match your research interests and graduate program requirements.

    The Thesis Advisor Shortlist view lets you compare department affiliations, advising status, and recent publications for the faculty you have shortlisted.

    Planning courses with guidance

      Publications & Reading List

      Each faculty profile includes a Publications tab listing recent and selected works such as journal articles, conference papers, book chapters, and books.

      You can search within a faculty member’s publications by title keyword and filter by publication type or year to focus on the most relevant work.

      Saving and exporting citations

      Use “Save to Reading List” to collect items across multiple faculty members, then open the Reading List to review or export citations.

      If citation export is enabled, you can choose styles such as BibTeX or APA on the export page before downloading.

      Additional tips

        Office hours & contacting faculty

        Most faculty profiles include office locations, phone extensions, and email addresses, along with dedicated Office Hours listings when available.

        Office hour slots can be filtered by day of the week and time range, helping you find options that fit your schedule.

        If booking is supported, use the “Book” control next to an available slot to request a meeting. Bookings are tracked internally and may send confirmations.

        Communication tips

          Header tools: My Notes & Contacts

          The site’s header links such as “My Notes” and “Contacts” open lightweight workspaces for your own reference. These tools are designed to complement the Reading List, Planned Courses, and Thesis Advisor Shortlist.

          My Notes

          Use My Notes to capture free-form thoughts about faculty, courses, or projects. You can paste URLs from faculty profiles or publications directly into a note so you can return to them later.

          Contacts

          The Contacts tool lets you record details for faculty and other academic connections, including office locations, phone extensions, and email addresses. You can optionally associate a contact entry with a specific faculty profile.

          These tools do not send messages themselves; they help you organize information that you may then use in your email client or other systems.
