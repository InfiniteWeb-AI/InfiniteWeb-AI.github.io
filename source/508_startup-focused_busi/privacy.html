Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 15, 2025

On this page

1. Overview

This Privacy Policy explains how Startupvisors ("we", "us") collects, uses, and protects information when you browse our startup software reviews, comparison guides, and other resources on this site.

It covers data related to your use of tool listings, articles, the Startup Stack Wizard, saved tools, newsletter subscriptions, and comments. It also explains how cookies, analytics, and similar technologies operate on privacy.html and the rest of the site.

This policy is designed to be transparent and practical for startup teams evaluating business software. If you have questions, you can always contact us.

2. Data we collect

We collect information in three main ways: information you provide directly, information generated when you use the site, and information related to cookies and similar technologies.

2.1 Information you provide

  • Newsletter subscriptions: When you subscribe, we store the email address and selected topics (for example, CRM or project management) as a NewsletterSubscription record.
  • Comments: When you comment on an article, we store your name, email address, comment text, and moderation status as a Comment.
  • Contact messages: If you contact us via forms or email, we store the details needed to respond to your inquiry.

2.2 Data generated by your use of the site

  • Usage of tool listings and reviews: We may log which tool categories, tools (Tool records), and articles you view to understand which content is most useful.
  • Saved tools: When you bookmark tools, we create SavedTool entries that record which tools were saved and where the action originated (for example, from a listing or comparison page).
  • Startup Stack Wizard: When you complete the wizard, we generate a StartupStackRecommendation and related StartupStackRecommendationItem entries that reflect your answers and the recommended tools.

2.3 Technical and device data

  • IP address (truncated or anonymized where possible).
  • Browser type and version, operating system, and device type.
  • Referrer URLs and basic interaction events (for example, page views, clicks on internal links).

3. Newsletter subscriptions

You can choose to receive email updates about new tool reviews, comparison guides, and stack-building advice by subscribing to our newsletter.

When you subscribe, we store:

  • Your email address.
  • Your selected topics (for example, CRM, marketing automation).
  • Optional details such as your company size segment (for example, 1–10 employees).

We use this information to send you relevant content and to understand which topics are most valuable for similar readers. Subscription data is represented as NewsletterSubscription entries.

You can unsubscribe at any time using the link in each email. When you unsubscribe, we mark the subscription as inactive or remove it according to our retention practices, but we may keep limited records to comply with legal obligations.

4. Comments & community

Some articles allow you to leave public comments. Comments are intended to help other startup teams learn from real-world experiences with the tools we review.

When you submit a comment, we typically collect:

  • Your display name.
  • Your email address (stored but not displayed publicly).
  • Your comment text and the time it was submitted.
  • Optional metadata such as whether the comment is an author reply.

Comments are stored as Comment records linked to the relevant Article. We moderate comments and may remove content that is spammy, abusive, or off-topic.

5. Cookies & tracking technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to keep the site secure, remember your preferences, understand how our content is used, and improve our recommendations.

Cookies fall into the following categories:

  • Essential: Required for core site functionality and security. These cannot be switched off and do not store marketing data.
  • Functional: Remember your choices, such as saved filters or recently viewed tools, to make the site easier to use.
  • Analytics: Help us understand what content is used (for example, which tool categories, tools, or wizard results are most popular) so we can improve the site.
  • Marketing: Used in limited cases to measure the performance of campaigns that bring visitors to our content.

You can adjust your cookie and tracking preferences at any time using the control panel below or from any cookie banner that appears when you first visit the site.

To jump directly to your settings, use the button below:

6. Analytics & performance data

We use analytics tools to understand how visitors use the site, such as which tool categories are most popular, which comparison articles drive engagement, and how the Startup Stack Wizard is used.

Analytics data typically includes:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page.
  • Clicks on internal links and CTAs (for example, links to a specific Tool review).
  • High-level information about your device, browser, and approximate region.

We configure analytics tools to avoid collecting more information than necessary and, where possible, to aggregate or anonymize data. You can control whether analytics cookies are used in the Cookies & tracking section above.

7. Saved tools & Startup Stack Wizard data

Parts of the site let you curate and compare tools so you can assemble a startup software stack that fits your budget and team.

7.1 Saved tools

When you bookmark tools on listing pages, comparison views, or tool detail pages, we create SavedTool entries that record which tool was saved, when it was saved, and the context (for example, from the wizard results or a comparison table).

Saved tools are primarily used to show your personal shortlist in the Saved Tools view. Depending on implementation, they may be stored locally in your browser or associated with a session or account.

7.2 Startup Stack Wizard

When you complete the Startup Stack Wizard, we store your answers and the generated recommendations as StartupStackRecommendation and StartupStackRecommendationItem records. These help us:

  • Build and display a tailored set of tools for your startup profile.
  • Understand which combinations of tools are most common for different company sizes and industries.
  • Improve future recommendations and content.

We do not use wizard data to make automated decisions that have legal or similarly significant effects on you.

8. Your rights & choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights over your personal data, such as the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict certain processing. Regardless of where you are, we aim to give you clear choices about how your data is used.

8.1 Managing cookies and tracking

You can review and update your cookie preferences at any time in the Cookies & tracking section of this page. You can also adjust settings in your browser to block or delete cookies.

8.2 Email preferences

Each newsletter email includes an unsubscribe link. If you unsubscribe, we stop sending marketing and editorial emails to that address, though we may still send essential service emails if you have an account-based relationship with us.

8.3 Access, correction, and deletion

If you would like to access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you (for example, comment history or newsletter subscription details), contact us using the details below. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling certain requests.

9. Contact & questions

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, you can reach us using the contact form on our Contact page.

When you contact us, we will use the information you provide only to respond to your inquiry and to manage any follow-up actions that are required.
