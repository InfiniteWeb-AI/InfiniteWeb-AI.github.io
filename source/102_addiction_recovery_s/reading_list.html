Using your Reading List

This page gathers articles you have chosen because they felt useful, hopeful, or worth revisiting.

  • Open an article by selecting its title in the middle panel.
  • Use the View control above to see everything together or grouped by topic.
  • Remove items that no longer serve you; you can always explore new material in the Resources section.
  • Notice the small “Just added” markers – they highlight articles you recently saved.

You are free to move at your own pace. These articles are here for you whenever you are ready.

Reading safely

Some topics can stir up strong feelings. It is okay to pause or step back.

  • If an article feels overwhelming, stop reading and take a break. Grounding exercises or a short walk can help.
  • Keep crisis and hotline information nearby in case you need immediate support.
  • Revisit articles that feel practical and empowering; set aside those that increase anxiety or guilt.

About highlighted items

When you arrive here after saving an article, you may see a small tag such as Just added beside its title. This simply helps you find the piece you just saved. You can clear this highlight at any time.
