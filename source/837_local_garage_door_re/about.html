About Us

Clear Lake Garage Door is a local, family-run team focused on fast response times, transparent pricing, and repairs that put safety first for every home and small business we serve.

Company Background

Clear Lake Garage Door started as a single-truck operation focused on helping neighbors get their garage doors working safely again without surprise fees or rushed workmanship. Over the years we have grown into a small, dedicated team that still runs on the same principles: answer the phone, show up when promised, explain the work, and stand behind every repair.

Our technicians work exclusively on overhead doors and openers. That focus means we know the brands, the common failure points, and the safest ways to complete repairs on heavy, high-tension components like torsion springs and lift cables. Whether you need an emergency repair late at night or a planned upgrade to a quieter, better insulated door, we treat each visit like we are working on our own property.

Because we are local and independent, decisions are made here in Clear Lake, not in another city. You will see the same technicians around the neighborhood, and you can always call us directly if you have questions after a job is complete.

Local Presence & Service Areas

We focus our work in Clear Lake, Webster, League City, Friendswood, and surrounding communities so that we can offer same-day and often same-hour response on urgent issues. By keeping our service radius tight, we limit drive time and spend more of the day actually repairing doors.

Every truck is stocked with common parts for the brands we see most in this area, including standard torsion springs, extension springs, cables, rollers, hinges, track hardware, safety sensors, and residential openers. Repairs, New Doors & Maintenance

Our work is organized around three main types of service so you always know what to expect when you schedule an appointment.

Repair services. We handle everything from emergency repair when the door will not open or close, to focused services such as spring repair, cable and roller replacement, and safety sensor troubleshooting. Many of these are available as same-day and after-hours appointments so you are not stuck with a door that will not secure or a vehicle trapped in the garage.

New door installation. When it is time for a new garage door, we can help you compare insulated and non-insulated models, materials such as steel, wood, or composite, and options for single or double doors. Our installers measure, remove the existing door, install new track and hardware as needed, and test balance and safety systems before leaving.

Preventative maintenance. To reduce unexpected breakdowns, we offer annual safety inspections and maintenance service that include a full door balance check, lubrication, hardware tightening, opener force and travel testing, and sensor alignment. Regular maintenance can extend the life of springs and reduce noise during operation.

Whichever service you choose, we explain the recommended work and pricing before starting and provide a written summary when the job is complete.

Safety & Customer Protection

Our approach to garage door safety

Garage doors are among the heaviest moving components in most homes. Springs are under high tension, doors can fall out of balance, and misadjusted openers can create unsafe force on the door. Every service visit includes a safety review, even if you call us for a single issue such as noise or a broken remote.

Technicians follow a step-by-step checklist that covers door balance, track and hardware condition, spring and cable inspection, photo eye alignment, and opener force and travel settings. If we find any condition that is unsafe, we will explain it clearly and offer options before proceeding with additional work.

We never recommend do-it-yourself torsion spring adjustments or repairs involving cables under tension. When we see unsafe aftermarket modifications or improvised hardware, we explain why it is a risk and how we can correct it using manufacturer-approved parts.

In-home professionalism. All team members arrive in marked vehicles, wear company identification, and treat your property with care. That includes protecting finished surfaces where we work, cleaning up after the job, and testing the door with you before we leave.

If you have any questions about the safety of your existing door, opener, or hardware, we encourage you to reach out before attempting adjustments yourself.
