MISSION

Aim Rock Local Music Association exists to keep live music loud, local, and accessible. We connect independent artists, grassroots venues, and audiences through a year-round program of concerts, workshops, and community_jam events.

Our mission is to remove barriers to music-making: affordable tickets, free or low-cost workshops, and a welcoming space for first-time performers and seasoned bands alike.

Every ticket, membership, and donation directly supports rehearsal spaces, fair artist fees, youth music education, and inclusive all_ages shows.

HISTORY

Aim Rock started as a DIY collective in 2012, when a group of local bands and venue owners came together to save a beloved Main Hall from closing. What began as a one-off benefit concert grew into a volunteer-run association.

Over the years we have:

  • Hosted hundreds of concerts, community_jam nights, and family_event afternoons.
  • Launched a workshop program covering instruments from guitar and drums to voice and electronic production.
  • Built partnerships with neighborhood schools and youth centers to bring music into classrooms.
  • Introduced sliding-scale pricing and free events so cost is never the only barrier.

Today, Aim Rock is a registered non-profit association with an active membership base, a volunteer board, and a network of artists and venues across the city.

ROLE IN THE COMMUNITY

Aim Rock is a hub for the local music ecosystem. We work at the intersection of artists, audiences, schools, and small businesses to keep culture rooted in the neighborhood.

In practice, this means:

  • Programming a balanced calendar of concerts, workshops, and family-friendly shows throughout the year.
  • Offering community_jam sessions where musicians of all levels can experiment, collaborate, and meet new bandmates.
  • Partnering with independent venues to keep stages busy and bar staff employed, even outside peak seasons.
  • Running youth and school programs that introduce kids and teens to instruments, songwriting, and live sound.
  • Providing a platform for underrepresented voices in the local scene through curated showcases and mentorship.

We see live music as public culture, not a luxury. Our community focus shapes every decision we make—from the events we book to the way we structure our membership plans and donation campaigns.

IMPACT STORIES

From garage band to festival stage

Two years ago, a group of high-school friends booked their first community_jam night through Aim Rock. With mentorship from our workshop tutors and slots on mixed-genre bills, they built a following and recently opened the main stage at our summer festival.

Family events that welcome everyone

Through our family_event series, parents and kids experience live music together in a relaxed, daytime setting. Pay-what-you-can tickets and sensory-friendly shows mean more families can attend, including those who usually feel excluded from traditional venues.

Workshops that spark new careers

Our workshop program has helped aspiring musicians and sound engineers gain practical skills, perform in front of supportive audiences, and connect with mentors. Several former participants now teach their own classes or work professionally in the local scene.

These stories are possible because of members, volunteers, and donors who believe in local music.
