Privacy Policy

Aim Rock Local Music Association ("we", "us", "our") respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect personal data when you interact with our website, attend events and workshops, become a member, donate, or subscribe to our newsletter.

What information we collect

We collect information you provide directly to us, such as when you:

  • Sign up for our newsletter (e.g., email address, name, preferred genres, frequency).
  • Buy tickets or add items to your cart (e.g., contact details needed to complete your order).
  • Register for workshops and classes (e.g., name, email, instrument, skill level).
  • Sign up for or renew membership (e.g., contact details and communication preferences).
  • Make a donation to a specific campaign.
  • Contact us directly by email, phone, or other channels.

We may also collect limited technical information about how you use our website, such as IP address, browser type, device information, and pages visited. This is typically collected using cookies and similar technologies (see the Cookies section below).

How we use your information

We process personal data only when we have a legal basis to do so, such as your consent, our legitimate interests in running the association, or to perform a contract (for example, when you purchase tickets or register for a workshop).

We use information to:

  • Provide and manage events, workshops, and community jam sessions.
  • Send you newsletters and event updates according to your NewsletterSubscription preferences.
  • Manage your cart, ticket purchases, and access to events.
  • Maintain membership records and apply membership benefits such as ticket discounts.
  • Record your donations and provide receipts where appropriate.
  • Respond to your questions, feedback, or data-related requests.
  • Improve our website, programming, and communication.

Data sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We may share limited information with trusted service providers who help us operate the website, process payments, send email communications, or analyze anonymous usage. These providers are required to handle your data securely and only for the tasks we specify.

Retention

We keep personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described above, unless a longer retention period is required by law (for example, for accounting or tax purposes). Newsletter and membership data are kept while your status is active and for a reasonable period after you unsubscribe or cancel, as allowed by law.

Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict the use of your personal data, as well as to object to certain processing or withdraw consent where we rely on it. See the Contact & Data Requests section below for how to reach us.

Terms of Use

By using this website, purchasing tickets, registering for workshops, or becoming a member, you agree to these Terms of Use. If you do not agree, please do not use the site.

Website use

  • You agree not to misuse the site, attempt to interfere with its security, or use it for unlawful purposes.
  • You are responsible for keeping any account or email access you use with us secure.
  • Content on this site, including texts, images, and logos, is owned by Aim Rock or our partners and may not be copied or reused without permission, except where clearly allowed.

Events, tickets, and workshops

  • Event details (time, venue, lineup) may change. We will aim to inform ticketholders of significant changes.
  • Ticket availability and prices are managed per Event and TicketType and may change without prior notice.
  • Please arrive on time and follow any venue rules or safety instructions. We reserve the right to refuse entry or remove attendees who put others at risk or seriously disrupt events.
  • Some events and workshops have age, skill level, or capacity limits. These conditions are stated in the listing.

Membership responsibilities

  • Members must provide accurate contact details and update us when they change.
  • Membership benefits apply only to the person named on the MembershipPlan or MembershipSignup record.
  • Misuse of membership discounts (for example, reselling discounted tickets commercially) may result in cancellation of membership without refund.

Liability

We aim to provide accurate information and well-run events, but we cannot guarantee uninterrupted access to the site or that every event will proceed as originally advertised. To the extent allowed by law, we are not liable for indirect or consequential losses arising from your use of the site or attendance at events.

Cookies & Similar Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to make the site work, keep your session active, and understand how visitors use our pages.

Types of cookies we may use

  • Essential cookies: Required for basic site functions such as keeping your cart (Cart and CartItem data) active while you browse or remembering your language preferences.
  • Performance and analytics: Used to understand how many people visit, which events or artists are viewed most, and how users move through the site. These are usually aggregated and do not identify you directly.
  • Preference cookies: May store simple settings such as your preferred view or filters so the site feels familiar when you come back.

Managing cookies

You can usually control or delete cookies through your browser settings. Turning off some cookies may affect how the site functions, for example by clearing your active cart or logging you out of a session.

Email & Communication Policy

We use email and other communication channels to share information about events, workshops, membership, and your transactions with us.

Newsletters and updates

When you create a NewsletterSubscription, we store your email address, optional name, preferred genres, preferred event types, and your chosen frequency (daily, weekly, or monthly). We use this information to send you tailored updates about concerts, workshops, and campaigns that match your interests.

You can change your preferences or unsubscribe at any time using the link provided in our emails or by visiting your newsletter settings.

If you arrived here from your subscription settings and want to go back, you can return to newsletter settings once you have finished reading this section.

Transactional and service messages

We may send you transactional emails when you:

  • Complete a ticket order or update your cart.
  • Register for a workshop or RSVP to a community jam.
  • Join, renew, or cancel a membership.
  • Make a donation or update a recurring contribution.

These messages are necessary to complete your request (for example, sending order confirmations or workshop details) and are not considered marketing.

Communication preferences

For members and subscribers, we respect the communication preference options you choose (for example, email, phone, SMS, or none where applicable). Some essential notices related to safety, major program changes, or significant policy updates may still be sent regardless of marketing preferences.

Payments & Donations

This section explains how we handle ticket purchases, workshop fees, and donations processed through the website or at our venues.

Payment methods

We may support different payment methods, including card payments via external services, onsite card terminals, cash, and bank transfer. For online or external payments we work with certified payment providers who process your card details securely.

What payment data we store

  • For online payments, card details are typically handled by an external payment processor. We do not use the website to display or store full card numbers, card verification codes, or other sensitive payment data beyond what is needed for the transaction.
  • For each Donation or ticket purchase, we may store the amount, currency, payment method, status (pending, succeeded, failed), and relevant timestamps for accounting and audit purposes.
  • For recurring monthly donations, we keep the information necessary to manage the recurrence, as provided by the payment processor.

Security

We take reasonable steps to protect payment-related information, including using secure connections (HTTPS) on the site and working with providers that comply with industry security standards for handling card data.

Refunds and cancellations

Refund rules for tickets, workshops, and donations may vary by event or campaign. Specific terms will be stated on the relevant event page, donation campaign description, or during checkout where applicable.

If you came from the Support Us or donations page and want to continue contributing after reviewing this policy, you can return to the donations page at any time.

Membership Policy

Membership helps support Aim Rock and may include benefits such as discounted tickets, priority booking, or member-only events, as described in each MembershipPlan.

Information we collect for membership

When you complete a MembershipSignup, we typically collect your name, contact details, chosen MembershipPlan, communication preference (for example, email or phone), and status (such as pending, active, or cancelled).

Use of membership information

  • To administer your membership, including renewals and status updates.
  • To apply member benefits such as ticket discounts to eligible events.
  • To send member-related communications, including program updates and required notices.

Membership duration and renewal

Each MembershipPlan has a defined duration and price. Renewal rules, including whether renewal is automatic or manual, are explained when you sign up. Where automatic renewals are used, you will be informed in advance and can change or cancel your membership according to the terms provided.

Ending membership

You may cancel your membership according to the terms presented when you joined. After cancellation, we retain limited information for accounting, audit, and legal purposes, but we stop applying member benefits and sending member-only updates unless you continue to receive them through another relationship such as a newsletter subscription.

Contact & Data Requests

We welcome questions about these policies and requests regarding your personal data. This includes requests to access, correct, delete, or limit the use of information we hold about you, where applicable law provides these rights.

How to contact us

You can contact the association using the details published on this website. The Contact link in the site footer provides the most up-to-date information on how to reach us electronically or by post.

What we need from you

When you submit a data-related request, we may need to ask for additional information to confirm your identity and to help us locate the relevant records (for example, the email address you used for a NewsletterSubscription or the approximate date of a donation or ticket purchase).

Responses to data requests

We aim to respond to privacy and data requests within a reasonable time and in accordance with applicable regulations. Some requests may not be possible to fulfill in full where we must retain certain records for legal, accounting, or safety reasons, but we will explain this if it applies to your situation.
