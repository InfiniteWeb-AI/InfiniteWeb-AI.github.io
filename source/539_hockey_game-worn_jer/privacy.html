Cookies & Tracking

How we use cookies, logs, and analytics

We use cookies and similar technologies to keep the site running smoothly, remember your preferences as a collector, and understand how people use the site so we can improve it. We do not sell your browsing history or use third-party ad networks on this hobby site.

When you visit pages such as team hubs, marketplace listings, articles, or your collections, our servers may log information such as your IP address, browser type, approximate location inferred from IP, and timestamps. These logs are used for security, abuse prevention, and high-level usage statistics.

Categories of cookies and trackers

  • Strictly necessary: Session cookies that keep you signed in, maintain CSRF protection, and remember basic site settings (for example, whether you are viewing prices in a certain currency). These are required for the site to function.
  • Preferences: Cookies that remember non-essential choices, such as preferred league filters or sorting options on marketplace and article lists.
  • Analytics: First-party or privacy-focused analytics tools that help us measure page views (for example, which guides are most read) and basic engagement. Data is generally aggregated and not used to build personal advertising profiles.

Managing cookies and analytics

You can typically control cookies at your browser level by blocking or deleting them, or by using private browsing modes. Blocking strictly necessary cookies may prevent you from signing in or using features like saving notes, lists, or RSVP responses.

Where we offer analytics or tracking choices on this site (for example, a banner or link labeled "Cookie settings"), those controls are described in the relevant interface and may allow you to opt out of certain metrics while still using the core site.

Notifications & Preferences

Email alerts, comment replies, and other notifications

We use notifications to keep you informed about site activity that you have opted into or reasonably expect based on your actions. Typical examples include alerts about new marketplace posts that match your interests, new authentication or collecting guides, or replies to your comments.

Types of notifications we may send

  • Profile-based preferences: If your profile includes settings such as 'notify_new_marketplace_posts' or 'notify_authentication_guides', we may send you emails or on-site notifications when relevant content is published.
  • Comment reply alerts: When you leave a comment and select an option like "Notify me of replies", we may email you when someone responds to that specific comment thread.
  • Event and RSVP updates: If you submit an RSVP for an event (such as a meetup or trade show), we may send you confirmation messages and important event updates.
  • Administrative messages: Infrequent, non-promotional messages about critical changes to this policy, security issues, or account-related matters.

Where you control notification settings

  • Profile settings: High-level notification switches (for example, for marketplace posts or new authentication guides) are typically located on your Profile Settings page.
  • Per-comment options: Comment forms on articles and marketplace listings may include a checkbox to receive or disable reply notifications for that specific thread.
  • Event RSVP forms: Event RSVP forms may include fields that clarify how you will be contacted about that event.
  • Unsubscribe links: Certain notification emails may include an unsubscribe or "manage preferences" link in the footer.

Data Types & Handling

What data we collect and how we use it

This section provides a high-level overview of the main categories of data used on Game Worn Jerseys. It focuses on concepts and practices, not on any specific collector's data.

Profile preferences and account basics

Profile information often includes your username, your favorite team, your preferred collecting era, an optional budget range, and your notification preferences (for example, whether you want alerts about new marketplace posts or new authentication guides). This information helps the site personalize content and notifications without exposing your actual preferences or identity to other users unless specifically described elsewhere (such as in a public profile view, if implemented).

  • Purpose: Enable sign-in, personalize experiences (such as highlighting teams or eras you favor), and store your notification choices.
  • Storage: Stored in a profile record linked to your account.
  • Retention: Kept for as long as your account exists or until you request deletion or anonymization, subject to legal obligations.

Comments on articles and listings

When you comment on an article or a marketplace listing, the site stores the comment text, your chosen display name, and metadata such as timestamps. Comments may also record whether you asked to be notified of replies.

  • Purpose: Power community discussion, questions, and fake-spotting conversations around jerseys and guides.
  • Visibility: Comments are usually public on the relevant article or listing page. Notification flags (for example, 'notify_replies') are not public.
  • Retention: Comments form part of the site's historical record and are generally retained; in some cases they may be removed for policy violations or upon request, where legally permitted.

Personal notes and annotations (overview)

Personal notes let you record your own research, wish lists, or trade ideas (for example, links to the oldest Lemieux article you found). These notes are tied to your account but are not intended to be visible to other users.

  • Purpose: Provide private workspace tools for collectors.
  • Visibility: Intended to be private to you unless clearly marked otherwise in a specific feature.
  • Retention: Kept until you delete them or delete your account, subject to backups and technical logs.

Saved lists, collections, and watchlists

Lists (such as reading lists, favorites, watchlists, wishlists, or custom jersey collections) store references to content like articles or marketplace listings. They typically do not duplicate the full content, but instead link to it via internal IDs.

  • Purpose: Let you organize material, flag favorites, or track items you might want to buy or trade.
  • Visibility: Lists are usually private by default unless a feature clearly indicates that a list is public or shareable.
  • Retention: Lists and list items are kept until you remove them or delete your account; orphaned references may be cleaned up automatically.

Direct messages and private conversations (overview)

Direct message features, where available, allow you to send private messages to other usernames. Messages may include text and links to articles or listings you want to discuss.

  • Purpose: Facilitate one-to-one communication, such as negotiating trades or sharing research.
  • Visibility: Visible to the sender, recipient, and, in limited cases, the site operators for security, abuse handling, or troubleshooting.
  • Retention: Retained for a period consistent with security and abuse-prevention needs; some interfaces may let you delete or archive specific conversations.

Events and RSVP responses (overview)

When you RSVP to an event, the site stores your submitted name, email address, RSVP status (for example, interested or going), and timestamps, linked to the event record. This helps organizers manage capacity and communicate updates.

  • Purpose: Coordinate meetups, trade nights, and other collector events.
  • Visibility: RSVP details are generally not posted publicly; organizers may see lists of responses.
  • Retention: RSVP data may be kept for a reasonable period after the event for follow-up and reporting, then archived or anonymized where feasible.

Marketplace listings and activity

Marketplace listings contain information you provide about jerseys or related items, including titles, descriptions, prices, teams, eras, and optional images. Listings may also accumulate comment counts and basic interaction metrics.

  • Purpose: Facilitate buying, selling, or trading game-worn and related jerseys.
  • Visibility: Listings are generally public; do not include information you consider private in listing titles or descriptions.
  • Retention: Listings and associated data may remain visible historically even after closing or sale, though certain details may be archived or removed over time.

At a glance: We aim to collect only the data needed to run this community, keep it secure, and improve the experience for serious jersey collectors. Whenever a form collects personal data, the fields and their intended use are described near that form.

Notes & Personal Data

Personal notes and private annotations

Personal notes are intended to be private to you unless a specific feature explicitly states otherwise.

Many collectors like to track research, authentication clues, and trade ideas in one place. The notes feature lets you store text entries that may include URLs, annotations about specific jerseys, or reminders for future hunts.

How your notes are treated

  • Association: Each note is linked to your account but is not displayed publicly on articles, listings, or profiles unless clearly indicated by the interface.
  • Content: Notes can contain any text you enter. Please avoid including highly sensitive personal information (such as government IDs or full payment card numbers).
  • Storage and security: Notes are stored on our servers with the same general technical and access controls used for other account-related data.

Editing and deleting notes

Where supported in the interface, you can create, edit, and delete your own notes. Deleting a note generally removes it from active systems; backups and logs may retain copies for a limited period for integrity and recovery purposes.

If you believe a note contains information that should be removed more aggressively for privacy reasons, you can contact us using the methods described in the Your rights & contact section.

Lists & Collections

Saved lists, collections, watchlists, and wishlists

Lists help you organize reading, track favorite jerseys, and monitor marketplace items you might want to buy or trade. A list typically stores references to articles or listings rather than duplicating the full content.

  1. Types of lists

    Examples include system lists such as a reading list or favorites, as well as user-created collections like 'Research - Leagues', watchlists, or wishlists.

  2. Visibility

    Lists are treated as private by default unless the interface clearly marks them as public or shareable. If we introduce public collections, those interfaces will show you exactly what will be shared.

  3. Use of list data

    Aggregated list data (for example, "how many users saved this guide") may be used to improve recommendations or highlight popular content, but individual list contents are not published without your clear action.

  4. Retention

    Lists and their items are kept until you remove them or your account is deleted. If the underlying article or listing is removed, the list entry may become an orphaned reference and may be cleaned up automatically.

Communications & Messages

Direct messages, contact forms, and other communications

On-site direct messages

Some areas of the site allow you to send direct messages to other usernames, often to share a listing, discuss a trade, or ask a question about an article. These messages contain the text you type and may include references to specific articles or listings.

  • Visibility: Messages are visible to the participants in the conversation and, in limited cases, site operators handling abuse reports, spam, or technical issues.
  • Use: Used solely to deliver communications between users and to maintain a safe community (for example, by responding to reports of harassment or fraud).

Contact forms and support requests

When you contact the site operators using a contact form, we collect the information you provide (such as your name, email, and message text) along with metadata such as timestamps and, in some cases, IP address for abuse prevention.

  • Purpose: Respond to your questions, handle bug reports, and address privacy or abuse concerns.
  • Retention: Support conversations may be stored for a reasonable period so we can reference past issues and improve our processes.

Abuse reports and safety

To keep the community safe, we may collect and review reports about problematic content or behavior. This can involve reviewing messages, comments, or listings implicated in the report, along with limited account information needed to investigate.

  • Purpose: Enforce site rules, prevent fraud, and protect users from harassment or scams.
  • Access: Restricted to authorized operators or moderators on a need-to-know basis.
  • Retention: Records related to abuse or security incidents may be kept longer than ordinary content, consistent with legal and safety obligations.
Events & RSVPs

Meetups, signings, and attendance information

Events such as trade nights, meetups, or shows are an important part of jersey collecting. When you interact with event listings or RSVP forms, certain information is collected to help organize those events and keep participants informed.

Event-related data we may collect

  • Event title, description, and location details.
  • Your submitted name and email address for RSVP purposes.
  • Your RSVP status (such as interested, going, or not_going).
  • Optional details you choose to provide (for example, jerseys you plan to bring).

How we use this information

  • To confirm attendance and manage capacity at venues.
  • To send event updates, schedule changes, or important logistical details.
  • To generate high-level statistics about event interest and participation.
  • To improve future events based on aggregated feedback and attendance.

We do not publish your RSVP details or contact information as part of public event pages without your clear consent. Organizers may see lists of attendees and contact details solely for event management purposes.

Some events may be hosted or co-hosted with third parties (for example, a venue or show organizer). Where that is the case, event descriptions will indicate when your information is shared with those parties and for what purposes.

Your Rights & Contact

How to access, update, or delete your data

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have rights relating to your personal data, such as the ability to access, correct, delete, or restrict certain processing. Even where these rights are not mandated, we aim to handle reasonable requests in a collector-friendly way.

Your possible rights

  • Access: Request confirmation of whether we process personal data about you and, where appropriate, a copy of that data in a readable format.
  • Correction: Ask us to correct or update inaccurate or incomplete information, such as your profile details.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of certain data, for example, by closing your account or asking that specific content linked to you be removed, subject to legal and operational constraints.
  • Restriction or objection: In some cases, object to particular uses (such as certain analytics or notifications) or ask us to limit processing.
  • Portability: Where applicable, request a copy of certain data in a portable format.

Requests may be subject to identity verification and to technical, legal, or contractual limitations (for example, records we must retain for security or legal reasons, or content that other users rely on, such as discussion threads).

How to contact us about privacy

If you have questions about this policy, want to exercise your data rights, or need to report a privacy concern, you can reach us using the site's contact channels.
