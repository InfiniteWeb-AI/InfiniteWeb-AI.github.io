Privacy Policy

IMPORTANT · Please read this policy carefully

How we handle your data

Effective date: 1 March 2024 · Version: 2024-03 (current)

On this page

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Diversity Visa Guidance ("we", "us") collects, uses, and protects personal data when you browse our website, explore immigration pathways, use tools such as the eligibility calculator and document checklists, save pathway plans, bookmark visa guides or FAQ articles, add services to your cart, and book consultations.

This page describes our practices at a high level. It does not read, modify, or expose any of your personal data or account-specific information. Instead, it explains in plain language how your data may be handled in the parts of the site that do process it.

2. Who we are and scope of this policy

Diversity Visa Guidance provides immigration consulting and visa guidance services, including online resources and self-service tools. This policy applies to personal data processed through this website and related services, including:

  • Service and consulting package information and purchases;
  • Consultation scheduling and bookings;
  • Personalized document checklists and checklist items;
  • Eligibility calculations for skilled visa pathways;
  • Saved immigration pathway plans and comparisons;
  • Bookmarks and favorites for visa guides and FAQs;
  • Shopping cart and checkout sessions.

3. Data we collect

The specific data we collect depends on how you use the site and services. In general we may collect the following categories of data:

3.1 Information you provide directly

  • Contact details such as name, email address, and phone number when you request information, create a checklist, begin a checkout session, or book a consultation.
  • Case-related information you provide when completing forms or questionnaires, such as target destination, pathway preferences, and general background details.
  • Consultation booking data, including preferred dates, time zones, and any notes you share to prepare for the consultation.

3.2 Data generated by tools and features

  • Document checklists you create using our checklist tool (for example, instances of DocumentChecklist and ChecklistItem data models).
  • Eligibility calculator runs (for example, records of EligibilityCalculationRun) including age, education level, work experience bands, and language test selections.
  • Saved pathway plans and comparisons (such as PathwayPlan and SavedPathwayPlan objects) used to help you track options over time.

3.3 Usage, device, and cookie data

When you visit our site, we may collect limited technical data such as your browser type, general region, and interactions with pages and tools. This is typically collected via cookies and similar technologies as described in the Cookies and consent section.

4. Cookies, tracking technologies, and consent

We use cookies and similar technologies to provide core functionality, improve the site, and remember your preferences. Where required by law, we will request your consent before using non-essential cookies.

4.1 Types of cookies we may use

  • Strictly necessary cookies that enable core features such as maintaining your shopping cart (Cart and CartItem state) during a browsing session or keeping you signed into tools where available.
  • Preference cookies that remember choices such as your selected country or pathway filters for discovering relevant ServicePackage or VisaGuide content.
  • Analytics cookies that help us understand how visitors use the site so we can improve content and tools. Where required, these cookies are only used with your consent.

4.2 How consent works on this site

When you first visit, or when relevant settings change, you may see a notice or banner asking you to review cookie categories and grant or refuse consent. Your choices may be stored in a cookie or similar mechanism so that they can be honored on future visits.

You can revisit or adjust your consent preferences at any time using your browser settings and, where available, our consent settings flow linked in the Consent management panel below.

5. Saved plans, bookmarks, and favorites

Our tools allow you to save and organize content so you can return to it easily. These features may involve the following types of personal data:

  • Saved pathway plans and comparisons, represented by PathwayPlan and SavedPathwayPlan, which may include your chosen destination country, pathway type (for example, canada_express_entry), fee and timeline estimates, and free-form notes.
  • Visa guide bookmarks (for example, GuideBookmark associated with a BookmarkFolder like visa_guides) that record which guides you have saved and when.
  • FAQ favorites (for example, FaqFavorite) that track which answers you have marked as particularly relevant.

These records may be stored in our systems or, in some cases, in your browser, in order to provide a consistent experience across visits. This privacy page itself does not inspect, load, or modify your saved plans, bookmarks, or favorites; it only explains how these features typically work.

6. Shopping cart and consultation bookings

When you add services or consultations to your cart or confirm a booking, we process data needed to fulfill your request.

6.1 Cart and checkout

Your cart is represented by a Cart record and individual CartItem entries. These may include which ServicePackage or ConsultationType you selected, quantities, prices, and any preferences such as preferred date ranges for consultations. During checkout, a CheckoutSession may link your cart to contact details you provide.

6.2 Consultation scheduling and bookings

When you schedule a consultation, we create or update related ConsultationType, ConsultationSlot, and ConsultationBooking records. These typically contain:

  • Your name, email address, and phone number;
  • The consultation type (for example, general_visa_strategy_30_min);
  • Your selected time slot and time zone;
  • Status updates such as pending, confirmed, cancelled, or completed.

We use this information solely to provide the consultation you request, manage changes, and keep appropriate records for administration and compliance.

7. Document checklists and eligibility tools

7.1 Document checklists

Our checklist tool uses DocumentChecklistTemplate and DocumentTemplateItem records as a starting point. When you personalize a list, we may create DocumentChecklist and ChecklistItem records to track which documents you plan to gather, target dates, and completion status.

These entries may contain limited personal data, such as country and visa type, marital status, and number of dependents, since these factors influence which documents are relevant.

7.2 Eligibility calculator

When you run an eligibility calculation, we may store an EligibilityCalculationRun that reflects the information you enter, such as:

  • Destination country and pathway key;
  • Age band, education level, and work experience band;
  • Language test type and self-assessed English proficiency level;
  • Calculated total points and eligibility outcome.

These records are used to provide you with results, improve the tool over time, and, where you request it, help our consultants prepare for follow-up discussions. This privacy page does not read or display individual eligibility runs.

8. How we share your data

We do not sell your personal data. We may share it only in the following situations:

  • Service providers who support our operations (for example, secure hosting, payment processing, and scheduling tools) under contracts that require appropriate protection of your data.
  • Professional obligations where our consultants must keep records for regulatory, tax, or audit purposes.
  • Legal requirements where we are obligated to respond to lawful requests from courts, regulators, or law enforcement.
  • With your instruction, for example where you ask us to share information with a third-party advisor or representative.

9. Data retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to provide the services you request, support site functionality, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

Retention periods may vary depending on the type of record. For example:

  • Consultation records and related ConsultationBooking data may be retained for a period required by professional or tax regulations.
  • Saved pathway plans, bookmarks, favorites, document checklists, and eligibility runs may be kept while your account or usage remains active and for a reasonable period afterwards, unless you request deletion where applicable.

10. Your privacy rights

Depending on your location and the applicable law, you may have some or all of the following rights over your personal data:

  • Access – to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction – to ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion – to ask us to delete your personal data when we no longer need it or where you withdraw consent, subject to legal obligations.
  • Restriction – to ask us to limit how we use your data in certain situations.
  • Objection – to object to certain types of processing, such as direct marketing, where applicable.
  • Portability – to request that we provide your data in a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format where technically feasible.

We will respond to requests in line with our legal obligations. For security, we may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request.

11. International data transfers

Because immigration and visa matters are inherently cross-border, your data may be processed or stored in countries other than your country of residence. We take appropriate steps to ensure that such transfers comply with applicable data protection laws and that your information remains protected to a comparable standard.

12. How to contact us about privacy

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle personal data, or if you would like to exercise your rights, you can contact us using the contact form on this site. Please indicate that your message is related to privacy or data protection.

To go directly to the contact form with privacy pre-selected, you can use the action links in the Your privacy rights section above or the contact link in the site footer.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our services, legal requirements, or how we process data. When we make material changes, we will update the version information above and, where appropriate, provide additional notice (for example, via a banner or email).
