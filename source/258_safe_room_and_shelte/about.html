Engineered safe rooms, proven in the field

Our mission

SafeGuard Rooms exists for a simple reason: when severe weather or security threats strike, every second and every detail matter. Our mission is to make code-compliant, rigorously engineered safe rooms and shelters accessible to households, businesses, and communities across the country.

We combine structural engineering, field-tested hardware, and a vetted installer network so that your safe room performs not just in theory, but in the real conditions it was built for.

Experience in safe rooms and shelters

Our team has decades of combined experience designing, manufacturing, and supporting storm shelters and safe rooms for high-risk regions. That expertise shows up in the details:

  • Residential safe rooms for new construction, remodels, and retrofits (including garage and interior installations).
  • Underground and above-ground storm shelters engineered for tornado and hurricane zones.
  • Commercial and community shelters sized for schools, healthcare, manufacturing, and municipal facilities.
  • Accessory packages for lighting, communications, ventilation, and emergency supplies.

If you are planning a complex project or integrating a safe room into a new build, our engineering team can collaborate directly with your architect, general contractor, or local officials.

Not sure which solution is right for your risk, site, and budget? You can always to talk through options with a specialist.

Certifications and safety standards

All active safe room and shelter models we offer are designed with recognized standards at the center of the process, not as an afterthought.

  • Design and testing guided by FEMA P-361 and ICC-500 criteria for extreme-wind safe rooms.
  • Use of rated doors, hardware, and anchoring systems tested for impact and cyclic loading.
  • Clear guidance on foundation requirements, site preparation, and inspection checkpoints.
  • Documentation to support local permitting and compliance where required.

Many projects also require coordination with local building departments or third-party engineers. Our team supports installers and customers with the documentation they need to keep approvals moving.

Who we serve

SafeGuard Rooms supports a range of customer types, each with different priorities and constraints:

  • Homeowners seeking protection for their families from tornadoes, hurricanes, and severe storms.
  • Home builders and remodelers integrating safe rooms into floor plans as a value-added feature and code-compliant refuge.
  • Businesses and facilities that need to protect employees, visitors, or critical operations with larger-capacity shelters.
  • Municipalities, schools, and public agencies delivering community-safe solutions in high-risk areas.

Each segment has different expectations around capacity, accessibility, and downtime. Our catalog and custom design options are structured to meet those needs without compromising on safety.

If your project does not fit cleanly into one of these categories, our team can still help. Share your requirements through the and we will review your project in detail.

How our consultation and installation process works

  1. Initial conversation. Share your location, structure type, and goals. We will help narrow down appropriate safe room or shelter types.
  2. Site review and recommendations. For many projects, a certified installer or partner will review photos, plans, or perform a site visit to confirm feasibility.
  3. Detailed quote. You receive a written scope including product, installation, and any accessories or options. For more specialized projects, you can also with engineering input.
  4. Installation and handoff. Our installer network follows documented installation procedures and checks anchoring, clearances, and operation before handoff.
