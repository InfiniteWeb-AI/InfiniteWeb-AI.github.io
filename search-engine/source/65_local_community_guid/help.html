Searching and filtering listings

Use search and filters on directory and events pages to quickly find places, services, and activities that fit your needs.

Search by ZIP code and radius

  1. Go to the relevant listing page (for example, Restaurants & Food, Parks & Outdoors, Events, or Volunteering).
  2. Enter your ZIP code into the search bar.
  3. Adjust the radius control to choose how far from that ZIP code you want to search (for example, 2, 5, 10, or 25 miles).
  4. Apply the search. The page shows only Place, Event, or Group results whose ZIP or service area overlaps your chosen radius.

If you do not enter a ZIP code, some pages may use a default community area and radius.

Filter by price and rating

  • Price filters use fields like average_price_per_person, hourly_rate, or an event's price. Choose a budget range (for example, free, $, $$, $$$).
  • Rating filters use the rating field (0.0–5.0 stars) and review_count. You can ask to see only highly rated options, such as 4.0+ stars.
  • Combine price and rating filters with your ZIP and radius to narrow down the list to your best options.

Filter by amenities and suitability

Amenities filters are based on specific fields on Place records, for example:

  • Family friendly – uses is_family_friendly (great for restaurants, parks, and childcare providers).
  • Playground – uses has_playground for parks.
  • Dog friendly – uses is_dog_friendly for parks and outdoor areas.
  • Takes reservations – uses takes_reservations for restaurants and cafes.
  • 24/7 emergency – uses is_24_7_emergency for trades like plumbers or electricians.

Select as many amenity filters as you like. Listings must satisfy each selected amenity in order to appear.

Filter by date and time

On events and some services, you can filter by specific dates and times:

  • Events & volunteering – filters use each Event record's start_datetime and end_datetime.
  • Services & childcare – availability filters use PlaceOperatingHour entries (day of week plus open and close times).
  1. Select your desired date from the calendar or date picker.
  2. Choose a time of day (for example, morning, afternoon, evening) or a specific time where available.
  3. Apply the filter to show only events happening or services open during that window.

If you see unexpected times, check that your device date/time and time zone are correct.

When you are ready to try these filters, go back to the Home page and open one of the directory or event lists.

Saving places and events into custom lists

Custom lists help you keep track of favorite places, upcoming events, and important contacts in one place.

What is a SavedList?

A SavedList is a named collection you create, such as:

  • Family Dinner Friday – restaurants you want to try.
  • Weekend Plan – parks and events for upcoming weekends.
  • Emergency Contacts – plumbers, electricians, and other 24/7 services.

Behind the scenes, each list is stored as a SavedList record with a name, optional description, and timestamps for when it was created and updated.

Creating a new list

  1. On any listing, event, or group page, look for a Save or Add to list action.
  2. Choose Create new list.
  3. Give your list a clear name (for example, 'Babysitters we like').
  4. Optionally add a short description to remind you how you use this list.
  5. Save. A new SavedList record is created and becomes available anywhere you can add items to lists.

Adding places, events, and groups

When you save something into a list, the site creates a SavedListItem record that connects your list to that item:

  • item_type – whether the item is a place, event, or group.
  • item_id – the internal ID of the Place, Event, or Group you saved.
  • position – an optional number that controls the order of items in a list (for example, highest priority first).
  • notes – your personal comments like 'kids loved this playground' or 'ask about vegetarian options'.
  1. On the item page, click Save or Add to list.
  2. Select an existing list (for example, 'Weekend Plan') or create a new one.
  3. Optionally add notes or adjust the order in the list later from the My Lists section.

Managing your lists

  • Open My Lists from the main navigation to see all of your SavedList entries.
  • Within a list, you can re-order SavedListItem entries, update notes, or remove items as your plans change.
  • Removing an item from a list deletes only the corresponding SavedListItem; the underlying place, event, or group remains available on the site.

To start building lists, head back to the Home page and open any category or event listing.

Joining groups and RSVP'ing to events

Use groups and RSVPs to stay involved with ongoing activities in your community.

Joining and leaving groups

Each group you see on the site comes from a Group record that describes its focus, typical meeting pattern, and location area.

  1. Open a group's detail page from the Groups & Clubs listing.
  2. Look for the Join group button.
  3. When you join, the site creates a GroupMembership record with status = 'member' and a joined_at timestamp.
  4. If you later leave the group, the same membership record is updated to status = 'left' and records a left_at time.
  5. Your group pages may show different options or messages depending on whether your membership status is member or left.

RSVP'ing to events and sessions

Events, volunteering opportunities, and group sessions are stored as Event records. When you respond to an event, the site tracks your response in an EventRSVP record.

  1. Open the event or volunteering opportunity you are interested in.
  2. Choose your response, such as:
    • Going – creates or updates an EventRSVP with status = 'going'.
    • Interested – sets status = 'interested' so you can easily find it later.
    • Not going – sets status = 'not_going' without removing the event from the site.
  3. If you need to cancel, look for a Cancel RSVP option. This sets the RSVP status = 'cancelled' while keeping a record that you responded in the past.
  4. Event pages may show how many spots are left using spots_total and spots_remaining.

Group sessions vs one-off events

  • Some events are one-time activities, like a neighborhood cleanup.
  • Others are recurring group sessions linked to a Group using the event's associated_group_id. RSVPing for these events helps group organizers plan ahead.

Contacting event organizers

Sometimes you may have a question about an event or volunteering opportunity before you RSVP or attend.

Using the Contact organizer form

  1. On an event or volunteering detail page, look for a Contact organizer button or link.
  2. This opens a small form where you can enter your name and a message.
  3. When you submit, the site creates an OrganizerMessage record that includes:
    • eventId – the event you are asking about.
    • sender_name – the name you entered.
    • message_text – your question or comment.
    • sent_at – when the message was sent.
    • status – whether the message was 'sent' successfully or 'failed'.
  4. If the message is sent successfully, you should see a confirmation on the event page.

Getting help with the site itself

If your question is about how to use the site (not a specific event organizer), please use the general Contact page instead. The Contact form creates a SiteContactMessage that goes to the site owner or administrator.

Troubleshooting common issues

I see no results

  • Check your ZIP code – a mistyped ZIP can easily hide all nearby results.
  • Try increasing the radius filter so the search covers a wider area.
  • Clear some filters (like price, rating, or amenities) in case the combination is too restrictive.
  • For events, make sure the date filter covers upcoming days rather than dates in the past.

Filters do not behave as I expect

  • Filters are usually combined with an AND rule. For example, choosing 'family friendly' and 'has playground' shows only places where both is_family_friendly and has_playground are true.
  • Some filters only apply to certain categories. For example, is_24_7_emergency applies to trades and home services, not parks.
  • If a filter does not apply to the page you are viewing, it may be hidden or disabled.

Times or dates look wrong

  • Check that your device's time zone, date, and time are set correctly.
  • Event times are based on each Event's start_datetime and end_datetime. If an event was updated recently, try refreshing the page.
  • If you think an organizer posted an incorrect time, you can use the Contact organizer feature on the event page to let them know.

My saved lists do not look right

  • If an item disappears from a list, it may have been removed from that specific SavedListItem, but the underlying place, event, or group is usually still available on the site.
  • Changes to a place or event (for example, a new address or updated time) are reflected automatically wherever it appears, including in your lists.
  • If you see duplicate items, you may have more than one SavedListItem pointing to the same place or event; you can safely remove extras.
