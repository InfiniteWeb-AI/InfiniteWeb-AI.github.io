Understand your coverage options

Here's how basic coverage compares to full value protection in a typical local move.

  • Often included at no or low additional charge as a baseline protection.
  • Coverage is typically based on the weight of your shipment, not each item's replacement cost.
  • Claims are usually paid at a fixed amount per pound per item, which may be less than the item's market value.
  • Can be appropriate for smaller moves or shipments with lower overall replacement value.
  • Aims to cover the cost to repair, replace, or reimburse for items that are lost or damaged during the move, subject to plan terms.
  • You select an insured value for your shipment and pay a premium linked to that value or your move estimate.
  • Well suited for higher-value items such as electronics, artwork, heirlooms, or new furniture.
  • May include deductibles, exclusions, or special limits, so it's important to review plan details before booking.

Tips for choosing a coverage level

  • Walk through each room and estimate what it would cost to replace major items at today's prices.
  • Include electronics, appliances, and specialty items you plan to move, not just furniture.
  • Think about what you'd be comfortable covering out-of-pocket versus what you want a plan to protect.
  • If you're unsure, it's generally safer to round your insured value up rather than down.
